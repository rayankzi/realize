# Design Principles

Good design isn't about decoration. It's about making things work well and feel inevitable.

> "Good design is as little design as possible. Less, but better — because it concentrates on the essential aspects, and the products are not burdened with non-essentials."
> — Dieter Rams

## The principles

**Clarity over cleverness.** Every element should have a clear purpose. If something doesn't help the user, remove it.

**Consistency builds trust.** Patterns should be predictable. When things work the way people expect, they feel confident and move faster.

**Start with content.** Design around the information people need, not the other way around. Typography, spacing, and hierarchy do most of the heavy lifting.

These aren't rules — they're defaults. Break them when you have a good reason.
