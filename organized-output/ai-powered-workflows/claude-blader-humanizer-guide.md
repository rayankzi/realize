# Blader Humanizer: The Complete Guide to Making AI Text Sound Human in Claude

**Topic/Category:** AI Tools, Writing, Productivity
**Primary Goal of the Post:** A comprehensive tutorial on installing, triggering, and getting the most out of the "Blader Humanizer" Claude skill — a persistent skill that automatically rewrites AI-flavored text into natural, human-sounding prose.

## Executive Summary

AI-generated text is easy to spot thanks to robotic openers, hollow intensifiers, over-structured formatting, and passive voice. The Blader Humanizer is a Claude skill that detects and rewrites these patterns into natural, human-sounding prose — without changing the original meaning. This carousel walks through the full lifecycle: what the skill does, how to install it, how to trigger it with natural language, what specific transformations it makes, a real before/after example, and power-user tips for dialing in tone and audience. The final slide promotes a related guide on building Claude skills from scratch.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **AI text has recognizable "tells":** Robotic openers ("Certainly!"), filler intensifiers ("It is important to note"), over-structured bullet storms, and passive voice all signal AI-generated content and erode reader trust.
- **Blader Humanizer is a persistent skill, not a one-off prompt:** Once installed, it's always available in Claude without needing to re-explain anything. Just ask.
- **It uses natural language triggers:** No special syntax or slash commands needed — phrases like "humanize this," "make this sound natural," or "fix the AI tone" activate it.
- **It preserves meaning while transforming tone:** Original meaning, key facts, data, specified tone, and desired structure are always preserved.
- **Tone modifiers let you customize output:** You can specify audience and tone (e.g., "warm but professional," "casual and witty — Gen Z audience," "senior exec — no fluff").
- **Two rounds of iteration is usually enough:** The recommended workflow is paste, humanize, read aloud, spot anything stiff, refine, done.
