## Executive Summary

This carousel from @youngurbanproject presents seven structured AI prompts designed for use with Claude's CoWork feature to systematically analyze and improve local SEO performance. Rather than offering generic SEO tips, the post focuses on using AI to perform competitive intelligence — extracting patterns from top-ranking competitors' Google Business Profiles, reviews, photos, and content strategies, then translating those insights into actionable plans. The underlying philosophy is that modern SEO success comes from understanding what's already working in your niche and reverse-engineering it with data, not guesswork.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **AI is an SEO analyst, not just a content writer:** The prompts position Claude as a strategic research tool for competitive analysis, pattern recognition, and strategy building — not just for generating blog posts.
- **Local SEO and Google Business Profile (GBP) are the focus:** Five of the seven prompts directly involve GBP data — posts, reviews, photos, and ranking signals.
- **Observation before action:** Several prompts explicitly instruct Claude to avoid recommendations and focus purely on extracting patterns first, ensuring strategies are grounded in real data.
- **Competitor benchmarking is the foundation:** Every prompt uses competitor data as the baseline, making the output relative to what's actually working in a specific niche.
- **Structured output formats are enforced:** Prompts request tables, bullet points, and structured formats to ensure actionable, scannable results.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

> **Prompt #1 — Market Understanding:**
> Analyze my site {{MY_WEBSITE_URL}} + Google Business Profile to extract services, locations, ICP, USPs & trust signals. Then analyze competitors {{COMP1}}, {{COMP2}}, {{COMP3}} for services, positioning & strengths. Compare both to find gaps, unfair advantages & SEO opportunities. Return in structured bullet points.

> **Prompt #2 — Ranking Levers:**
> Based ONLY on top-ranking competitors, identify the top 7 ranking levers Google rewards for this keyword. Rank them by impact on map rankings (highest to lowest). For each lever, show which competitors demonstrate it and how. Avoid generic advice. Return as a table: Lever, Evidence, Why it matters.

> **Prompt #3 — GBP Content Reverse-Engineering:**
> Analyze Google Business Profile posts of {{COMP1}}, {{COMP2}}, {{COMP3}} for post types, frequency, themes, offers, CTAs, media and timing. Identify patterns linked to strong rankings and engagement. Then create a clear, actionable posting plan for my GBP with post types, frequency, themes and CTA style. Avoid vague advice.

> **Prompt #4 — Pattern Spotting:**
> Use the existing data from the top 3 competitors. Do not provide recommendations, fixes, or opinions. Focus only on extracting recurring patterns across them, including primary/secondary categories, review count ranges, photo posting frequency, and keyword usage in business names and descriptions. Report similarities only. Do not include insights, conclusions, or next steps. Keep the output strictly observational and structured.

> **Prompt #5 — Outliers & Dominant Signals:**
> Work from the existing competitor dataset. Do not provide recommendations, fixes, or opinions. Identify outliers in the ranking set, businesses that perform well despite fewer reviews, weaker branding, or other inconsistencies. Then isolate which ranking signals appear most influential for this keyword (e.g., proximity, review authority, category alignment, keyword presence, profile activity/freshness). Keep the output strictly observational, focused on patterns and anomalies only. No conclusions or next steps. Return in a structured format.

> **Prompt #6 — Review Strategy:**
> Review the Google Business Profile reviews of {{COMP1}}, {{COMP2}}, {{COMP3}}. Examine review volume, frequency over time, recency, rating distribution, and recurring keyword patterns across reviews. Analyze how keywords naturally appear in both customer reviews and owner responses. Based on these patterns, build a clear and practical review strategy outlining keyword themes to reinforce, ideal review pacing, and structured reply approaches. Keep the output specific and execution-focused. Avoid generic advice.

> **Prompt #7 — Photo Ranking Strategy:**
> Evaluate the Google Business Profile photos of {{COMP1}}, {{COMP2}}, {{COMP3}}. Assess total photo count, posting consistency, recency, and distribution across photo types (e.g., job-site, team, interior/exterior, before-after, branded visuals), including geo-relevance where visible. Identify recurring patterns among top-ranking profiles. Using these observations, create a practical photo upload strategy outlining priority photo types, ideal weekly posting cadence, and execution focus areas. Keep the output specific and actionable. Avoid generic suggestions.
