# What Nobody Tells You About Surviving Your First Tech Internship

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Tech Internships
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** To share honest, practical advice for tech interns — especially around communication and relationship-building — based on the creator's first-hand experience interning at Amazon at age 18.

## Executive Summary

The creator reflects on their Amazon internship experience, which they started at just 18 years old. Despite admitting their coding skills were average or even subpar, they secured a return offer — and they attribute that success almost entirely to soft skills. The core message: communication, relationship-building, and resourcefulness matter just as much (if not more) than raw technical ability. The reel debunks the myth that you need to be a coding prodigy to succeed and encourages nervous interns to lean into the "little things."

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Communication is half the job:** Being able to clearly articulate problems, progress, and questions is essential. Strong communication can compensate for weaker coding skills.
- **Build relationships beyond your mentor:** Your mentor will be your closest ally, but they won't always be available. Cultivating relationships with other team members ensures you always have someone to turn to.
- **Do your homework before asking questions:** There *are* stupid questions — specifically ones you could have answered yourself with a quick search. Use internal documentation and search engines before going to your mentor or coworkers.
- **Don't let fear hold you back:** The creator wasn't a strong coder and still got a return offer. With AI tools available now, the bar for getting through technical blockers is even lower.
