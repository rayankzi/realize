## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Snappy clip length is key:** Keep individual clips between 0.3–0.7 seconds for pacing, and trim montage intro clips to 0.1–0.2 seconds for an eye-catching opening.
- **Duplicate-and-move technique for intros:** Duplicate aesthetic clips with movement, move them to the beginning, and trim them short to create a rapid-fire montage before the main content starts.
- **Typing text animation + sound design:** Use CapCut's "Type One" animation on body text, pair it with the "loop keyboard" sound (found under Sounds, not Sound Effects), and cap it with a "click mouse" sound effect when the animation ends.
- **Voiceover in short overlapping segments:** Recording voiceover in small chunks prevents pauses, keeps energy up, and makes the video feel snappier. Slightly overlapping each segment eliminates dead air.
- **Export settings matter:** Use 4K resolution, 60fps, and turn OFF AI Ultra HD to preserve the highest quality output.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. **Open CapCut** and select "New Project." Import all video clips taken throughout the day.
2. **Split and trim clips** to 0.3–0.7 seconds each for a fast-paced edit.
3. **Create the snappy montage intro:**
   - Select an aesthetic clip with movement or one that previews the video's theme.
   - Hit "Duplicate."
   - Move the duplicated clip to the very beginning of the timeline.
   - Trim it down to ~0.2 seconds (or 0.1 seconds for extra snappiness).
   - Repeat with as many clips as feels right (the creator used about 9 for this video).
4. **Add text overlays:**
   - Click "Add Text."
   - Create a **main text** (e.g., "Day in the Life").
   - Create a **smaller body text** with additional context about the video.
5. **Animate the body text:**
   - Select the smaller text, go to "Animations," and choose **"Type One."**
   - Adjust the duration slider at the bottom to control typing speed (slower is preferred).
6. **Add the typing sound effect:**
   - Go to **Sounds** (not Sound Effects).
   - Search for **"loop keyboard"** — favorite it for quick access.
   - Trim the typing sound to end exactly where the typing animation stops.
7. **Add a click sound:**
   - Go to **Sound Effects** and add a **"click mouse"** sound effect right where the typing ends. This should align with where the main video content begins.
8. **Record voiceover:**
   - Record in short sections rather than one long take.
   - Slightly overlap each voiceover segment so there are no pauses between talking points.
9. **Export at highest quality:**
   - Click next to the export button on "AI UHD" to open settings.
   - Set resolution to **4K**.
   - Set frame rate to **60fps**.
   - Turn **OFF** AI Ultra HD.
   - Export.
