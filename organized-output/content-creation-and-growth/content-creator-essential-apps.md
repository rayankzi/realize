# 10 Must-Have Apps for Running a Content Creation Business

**Topic/Category:** Content Creation / UGC Creator Tools
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To share the 10 essential apps a UGC and content creator uses daily to run her content creation business, covering everything from video editing to project management to email outreach.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel breaks down the complete tech stack of a full-time content creator after two years in the business. The creator shares her "ride or die" apps spanning video editing (CapCut), captioning (Captions), graphic design (Canva), scripting (Teleprompter), project management (Notion), social media management (Stanley), cloud storage (Google Drive), email tracking (Mailsuite), communication (Gmail), and professional branding (Signature Hound). Together, these tools form an end-to-end workflow for creating, organizing, publishing, and pitching content.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **CapCut is the primary video editor**, with a strong preference for the desktop version over mobile due to speed (10x faster) and the ability to click and group elements. Copyright-free music is a key feature for ad content.
- **Canva Pro is considered essential** — used for carousel design, creator portfolio hosting, and invoice creation with custom templates.
- **Notion serves as the operational backbone** — housing all data, collabs tracking, content calendars, post schedules, content idea databases, and a trial reels workflow (inspo → idea → caption → video link).
- **Stanley is the social media management tool** — acting as an IG agent to streamline content, 10x video idea output, organize content schedules, manage warning/flags, and track engagement and consistency.
- **Email infrastructure matters** — the creator uses a three-tool email stack: Gmail for communication, Mailsuite to track if emails are opened (informing follow-up timing), and Signature Hound to create a professional email signature.
- **Google Drive with 2TB storage** is used to store all created videos and b-roll footage for quick reuse, plus easy sharing with clients for review.
- **A teleprompter app is recommended** even for talking-head videos — the key tip is to practice sounding natural rather than reading robotically.
