## Executive Summary

This reel reveals a critical but overlooked issue with how Instagram handles external links: the in-app browser. When users tap a link in your bio or stories, Instagram opens it inside its own browser rather than the native app (e.g., YouTube). This means viewers aren't logged in, can't like, comment, or subscribe — tanking your engagement and conversions. The creator presents "deep links" as the fix, which force Instagram to open the destination in its native app instead of the in-app browser, and offers a free deep link generator tool via the comments.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Instagram intentionally keeps users inside its in-app browser to prevent them from leaving the platform — every standard link you share is affected by this.
- **Insight 2:** Traffic from Instagram's in-app browser leads to lower engagement and conversions because users aren't authenticated in the destination app (can't like, comment, subscribe on YouTube, etc.).
- **Insight 3:** Deep links bypass the in-app browser and force the actual native app to open, restoring full user functionality and dramatically improving engagement metrics.
