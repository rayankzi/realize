## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Natural sunlight is the single most impactful factor for video quality — prioritize it over artificial lighting setups.
- **Insight 2:** Shoot in 4K at 60fps using the back camera, but export in 1080p at 30 or 60fps for the best balance of quality and file size.
- **Insight 3:** Turn off HDR on iPhone (and Smart HDR in CapCut) to avoid washed-out, inconsistent color grading in your final export.
- **Insight 4:** Use the optical 2x zoom button rather than digital zoom, and experiment with macro lenses for extreme close-up detail.
- **Insight 5:** Slow, steady camera movements (and editing footage down to 0.8x speed) create a more polished, cinematic feel.
- **Insight 6:** Disable the automatic macro flower icon on newer iPhones to maintain manual control over your focus.
- **Insight 7:** When delivering content to brands, use Google Drive or WeTransfer instead of platforms that compress video quality.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

This is a tutorial-style reel. Here are the steps in order:

1. **Prioritize good lighting** — Position yourself near natural sunlight. This is the foundation of high-quality footage.
2. **Set your camera to 4K at 60fps** — Use the rear (back) camera, never the front camera, for maximum quality.
3. **Turn off HDR** — Go into iPhone camera settings and disable HDR. If editing in CapCut, also turn off Smart HDR on export.
4. **Use optical zoom** — If your iPhone has a 2x zoom button (or similar), tap it to engage the optical lens. Also consider testing a macro lens attachment for super close-up detail shots.
5. **Film slowly and steadily** — Move the camera slowly during your shots. In post-production, consider slowing footage to 0.8x speed for added smoothness.
6. **Disable the macro flower icon** — On newer iPhones, when the macro mode activates automatically (shown by a flower icon), tap it to turn it off and maintain control.
7. **Export properly** — Export your edited video in 1080p at 30 or 60fps from CapCut or a similar editor.
8. **Share without compression** — When sending content to brands, use Google Drive or WeTransfer to preserve full video quality.
