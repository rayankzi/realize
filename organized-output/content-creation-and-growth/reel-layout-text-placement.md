# Master the Reel Layout: Eye Zones, Safe Zones & Text Placement

**Topic/Category:** Content Creation / Social Media Tips
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach creators where to place visual elements (eyes, captions, text) on a reel to maximize readability and engagement, while avoiding dead zones in the frame.

## Executive Summary

This short-form video is a rapid-fire tutorial on visual composition for Instagram Reels (and similar vertical video formats). The creator maps out the frame into distinct zones — identifying where the viewer's eyes naturally land, where captions should be placed, and which areas to avoid entirely. They also share quick pro tips on punching in while keeping the eyeline consistent, and adding a stroke to text when the background is busy. The caption reinforces that this is part of a broader series of content creation tips.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** The frame has a natural hierarchy — the upper-center area is where viewers' eyes go first, making it the ideal spot for your face or focal point.
- **Insight 2:** Captions and text overlays should be placed in a designated "safe zone" (likely the middle portion of the frame), away from UI elements like the like/comment buttons and the bottom navigation bar.
- **Insight 3:** There is a "danger zone" and an area to "avoid at all costs" — these are regions obscured by platform UI or where the viewer's attention drops off (typically the very bottom and edges of the frame).
- **Insight 4:** When punching in (zooming/cropping in post), keep the eyeline consistent so the subject doesn't appear to jump positions between cuts.
- **Insight 5:** On busy or cluttered backgrounds, adding a stroke (outline) to your text ensures it remains legible.
