# Where to Place Text on Reels to Maximize Reach

**Topic/Category:** Instagram Growth / Content Creation
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Teach creators exactly where to position text overlays on Instagram Reels (both talk-to-camera and B-roll styles) to increase readability, watch time, and ultimately reach.

---

## Executive Summary

Most viral Reels follow a predictable text placement layout, and getting it right is a simple tweak that can dramatically boost performance. This carousel breaks down the two most common Reel formats — talk-to-camera and B-roll — showing exactly where text should go on each. It also reveals how to use Instagram's built-in "safe zone" guidelines in the Edits tool to ensure text is never covered by UI buttons or caption bars. The core insight: text placement isn't just about aesthetics — it directly impacts watch time, which drives the algorithm to push your content further.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Text placement follows predictable patterns on viral Reels.** Where your text sits matters more than most creators realize — it affects readability, scannability, and watch time.
- **Talk-to-camera Reels have a specific layout:** Hook text over your head (first 3 seconds), face centered on screen, and closed captions positioned under your face but still inside the safe zone.
- **B-roll Reels should have text centered on screen.** Avoid the very top and bottom edges so Instagram buttons and UI elements don't cover your text. Use the slider to resize text to fit comfortably in the center area.
- **Instagram Edits has a built-in "safe zone" feature.** Yellow guidelines appear when dragging text — yellow lines show center alignment, and yellow side bars indicate where Instagram's UI may cover your text.
- **More watch time = more views & reach.** Making content easier to read and scan directly translates to longer watch times, which signals the algorithm to distribute the Reel more widely.
