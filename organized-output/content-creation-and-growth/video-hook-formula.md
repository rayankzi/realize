# Why Your Videos Flop: The Hook Formula

**Topic/Category:** Content Creation / Social Media Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach creators why their videos underperform and how to craft strong hooks that stop the scroll in the first 1-3 seconds.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel delivers a concise masterclass on the most critical element of short-form video: the hook. The creator argues that most videos fail not because the content is bad, but because the opening 1-3 seconds don't earn attention. The caption expands on the spoken content with a detailed breakdown of hook strategies, emphasizing that attention is earned through pattern interruption — both visually and verbally. The core message is actionable: test different openings, repost with stronger hooks, and never assume your audience will stick around.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **The first 1-3 seconds decide everything.** If your hook doesn't interrupt the scroll, even great content won't get seen.
- **Attention is earned, not assumed.** Consistent creators understand that every video must re-earn the viewer's attention from scratch.
- **Strong hooks interrupt patterns, they don't beg.** The goal is to make the viewer's brain pause involuntarily.
- **A strong video needs both a visual hook and a spoken hook.** Relying on just one leaves performance on the table.
- **Your content might be fine — it just never got a fair chance.** Reposting with a stronger opening can unlock a video's potential.
