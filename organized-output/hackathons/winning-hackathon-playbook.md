## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Idea > Execution:** Half of winning teams had a great idea with only mediocre execution but a strong presentation. Spend real time developing your concept and articulating why it matters.
- **Sleep is a competitive advantage:** Despite hackathon culture glorifying all-nighters, getting good rest directly improves your final demo performance.
- **Go deep, not wide:** Stop trying to integrate every sponsor's API. Judges see through the "hit every base" strategy. Choose 1-2 technologies and 2-4 tracks max.
- **Build what you enjoy:** Passion drives better execution. Sam won multiple hackathons by making games in a sea of B2B SaaS products — because he genuinely enjoyed building them.

**Core Framework (4 Pillars):**

1. **Develop a fire idea** — Don't underestimate ideation. A strong concept with mediocre code and a great presentation beats a technically complex project with a weak pitch.
2. **Get sleep** — Reject the all-nighter culture. Rest pays dividends during final demos when clarity and energy matter most.
3. **Focus your scope** — Pick 1-2 technologies and 2-4 tracks. Depth over breadth. Judges recognize when teams are just checking sponsor boxes without real depth.
