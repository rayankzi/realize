# How to Win the Interview in the First 10 Minutes

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Interview Preparation
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach job seekers three specific conversational techniques to make a strong impression in the critical first 10 minutes of a job interview, based on the creator's experience on the hiring side.

---

## Executive Summary

Drawing from firsthand experience as a hiring insider, the creator reveals that MBB and big tech hiring managers often decide a candidate's fate within the first 10 minutes. The video lays out three actionable techniques — the Opening Hook, the Headline Technique, and the Feel-Good Technique — designed to build rapport, showcase personality, and leave a memorable impression before the formal questioning even begins. The caption reinforces that these are proven principles used to mentor early-career professionals into roles at McKinsey, Google, BCG, and similar firms.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Hiring managers at top firms (MBB, big tech) often know within the first 10 minutes whether a candidate is right for the role — first impressions are disproportionately important.
- **Insight 2:** The small talk before the "official" interview starts is not throwaway conversation — it's a strategic opportunity to build connection, exude energy, and subtly signal competence.
- **Insight 3:** Giving interviewers choices between stories (the Headline Technique) is described as an "unpatched hack" — it broadens your narrative surface area without consuming time on every example.
- **Insight 4:** Complimenting an interviewer's questions (when done organically) creates a positive emotional association with you as a candidate.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning (Hook):** The creator opens with a bold claim rooted in authority — "As someone who's now on the other side of hiring" — and states that hiring managers know within 10 minutes if you're right for the job. This immediately creates urgency.

**Middle (Three Techniques):**

1. **The Opening Hook** — When asked "How are you?", never say "good." Instead, deliver an energetic, personal micro-story that:
   - Exudes positive energy
   - Subliminally signals you're an accomplished person
   - Ends with an open question to continue the conversation and extend the rapport-building window

2. **The Headline Technique** — When asked a behavioral question (e.g., "Tell me a time you led a team"), offer two story options as "headlines" and let the interviewer choose. This:
   - Gives the interviewer more context about who you are
   - Makes you stand out from candidates who only share one story
   - Puts the interviewer in an active role, increasing engagement

3. **The Feel-Good Technique** — Compliment the interviewer's questions organically to make them feel good about the conversation (and by extension, about you). Use phrases like:
   - "Whoa, that's a really hard question. I don't think I've ever been asked that."
   - "Yeah, I love that question. I think..."

**End (Wrap-Up):** The creator reinforces that the first 10 minutes are "make or break" and closes with a follow CTA.
