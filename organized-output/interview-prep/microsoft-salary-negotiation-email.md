# How One Email Turned a $178K Offer Into $222K at Microsoft

**Topic/Category:** Career / Salary Negotiation
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach new grads and early-career engineers how to negotiate a higher salary using a simple, data-driven email framework — no bluffing or competing offers required.

## Executive Summary

The creator shares a personal story of negotiating a Microsoft return offer from $178K to $222K total comp — a $44K increase — by sending a single, four-sentence email. The core message is that recruiters expect negotiation, first offers are always at the bottom of an internal band, and a calm, data-backed ask is all it takes to move the number up. The reel doubles as a mini-template for anyone facing a similar situation.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** The first offer is never the best offer. Companies have a compensation band and start at the bottom — your job is to move them up.
- **Insight 2:** You don't need a competing offer, an ultimatum, or emotional leverage. Market data and a clear target number are enough.
- **Insight 3:** Recruiters expect candidates to negotiate. Not negotiating leaves money on the table by default.
- **Insight 4:** A short, structured email (4 sentences) can be more effective than a drawn-out back-and-forth conversation.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

**4-Sentence Negotiation Email Framework:**

> 1. Thank them for the offer (genuine, not performative)
> 2. Reference market data for the role and level
> 3. State your target number clearly
> 4. Give them a reason (your contributions, competing interest, or simply the data)
