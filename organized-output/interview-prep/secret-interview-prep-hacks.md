## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** You can get practice interviews with people at the exact company you're interviewing at by filtering LinkedIn for alumni at your school or current company who work there, then reaching out with a short, specific message.
- **Insight 2:** Don't reinvent the wheel — study answer structures from YouTube channels like Exponent and Rocketblocks, talk to people who've landed the role before, and copy the frameworks that already work. Layer ChatGPT on top to practice with a "winning playbook."
- **Insight 3:** Secret Slack and Discord communities (Lewis Lin Slack Channel, Product Haven, Tech Interview Prep Discord) are a cheat code for exchanging interview questions, scheduling mock sessions, and hearing about open roles.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Hack 1 — Mock Interview with the Company:**
1. Open LinkedIn and apply filters: Your School or Current Company + Location + Target Company + Target Role.
2. Reach out via LinkedIn message or email using the provided template (see Verbatim Templates below).
3. Convert the initial informational chat into a mock interview — school alumni are especially willing to help.

**Hack 2 — Copy Answers That Work:**
1. Watch interview walkthrough videos on YouTube (Exponent, Rocketblocks).
2. Copy the *structure* and storytelling style, not the exact words.
3. Do mock interviews with people who have already landed the job and ask what worked for them.
4. Feed all your prep materials into ChatGPT and use it as a practice partner with a proven framework.

**Hack 3 — Join Secret Communities:**
1. Search for and join Slack/Discord communities focused on interview prep.
2. Recommended communities: Lewis Lin Slack Channel, Product Haven, Tech Interview Prep Discord.
3. Use these to exchange interview questions, find mock interview partners, and discover which companies are actively hiring.
