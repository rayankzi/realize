## Executive Summary

A Cornell student reflects on landing a position at Jane Street at 19 years old — not to brag, but to help others avoid the same pitfalls. The core message is that overcommitting destroys quality, recruitment and school require different but equally consistent strategies, networking and community involvement are critical, and deliberate interview practice with journaling and feedback loops is what ultimately makes the difference. The caption reinforces this is part of a broader series on recruitment advice.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Overloading your schedule (research, conferences, recruitment, classes, work) doesn't make you more competitive — it degrades the quality of everything you produce. You pay for spreading yourself too thin.
- **Insight 2:** Recruitment and academics measure different things, but both reward consistency. Build strong projects and join teams that develop recruitable skills rather than cramming everything into interview season.
- **Insight 3:** Networking and community are force multipliers. The creator landed Jane Street specifically through connections made via the Underrepresented Minorities in Computing group at Cornell and the Afrotech recruitment conference.
- **Insight 4:** Treat interview prep like a class — practice deliberately, get feedback, journal your mistakes, and iterate until you stop making them. Ego is the enemy; embrace being told you're wrong.
- **Insight 5:** Pick your non-negotiables (sleep, key classes, one priority project/team) and treat everything else as optional. Ruthless prioritization beats brute-force time management.
