# Ranking Job Search Hacks: Resume Tips Tier List

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Job Search Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** A recruiter ranks common job search hacks and resume tips on a tier list (S through F), giving viewers actionable guidance on what actually works and what to avoid.

## Executive Summary

In this reel, the creator interviews recruiter Jerry Lee (@jerryjhlee) to rank popular job search hacks on a tier list. The key takeaway is that authenticity and smart strategy beat gimmicks — tailoring your resume to a job title (not every posting), leveraging AI tools, and honestly amplifying your impact are all high-tier moves, while tricks like white-font ATS hacking and over-tailoring per job description are wastes of time. The advice is practical and challenges several widely-shared resume tips circulating on social media.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Tailor your resume to a **job title**, not to every individual job description. Per-posting customization is overkill and ranked F tier.
- **Insight 2:** Apply when you meet roughly **75% of qualifications** — if you meet 100%, you've waited too long. Don't self-select out of roles where you're a strong partial fit.
- **Insight 3:** **AI is S tier** for job searching. Use it to apply faster, build stronger resumes, and prepare for interviews. Manual-only job searching is falling behind.
- **Insight 4:** You can **amplify your impact** on your resume without lying. Don't fabricate companies or titles, but confidently claim your contributions to projects.
- **Insight 5:** **Education placement** depends entirely on your career stage — top of resume if you're a current student (S tier), bottom or removed if you graduated over a year ago (F tier).
- **Insight 6:** White font ATS tricks are F tier. A well-constructed resume naturally contains the right keywords. People hire people, not keyword-stuffed documents.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

- **Opening:** The creator introduces the premise — ranking job search hacks with recruiter Jerry Lee.
- **Hack 1 – Tailoring resume to every JD (F tier):** Jerry argues this is unnecessary. Tailor to a job title instead and keep a strong base resume.
- **Hack 2 – Applying without all qualifications (A tier):** The 75% rule is introduced. Meeting every qualification means you waited too long.
- **Hack 3 – Arial font (C tier):** Font choice should match the brand and messaging of your resume; Arial is fine but not a one-size-fits-all answer.
- **Hack 4 – Ethnic name on resume (A tier):** Strong stance — never change your name for a job. Be proud of who you are.
- **Hack 5 – Using AI (S tier):** Enthusiastic endorsement of AI for every stage of the job search. Everyone else is using it, so you should too.
- **Hack 6 – Lying on resume (B tier):** Nuanced take — don't lie about employers or titles, but embellishing your impact and role scope is normal and expected.
- **Hack 7 – Education at top (S or F tier):** Context-dependent. S tier for current students (recruiters need to know you're a student for internships), F tier for experienced professionals.
- **Hack 8 – White font ATS trick (F tier):** Dismissed entirely. A good resume already has the right keywords; gaming the system isn't necessary.
- **Closing:** Asks for audience opinions in the comments.
