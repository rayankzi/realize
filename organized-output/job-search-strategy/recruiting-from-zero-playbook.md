# If I Had to Recruit Again in 2026: A Senior's Complete Playbook

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Recruiting / College
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To share five actionable recruiting strategies from a college senior who successfully landed tech and startup offers, aimed at students starting from zero.

---

## Executive Summary

A University of Texas senior who landed both big tech and startup offers shares her top five lessons for anyone starting the recruiting process from scratch in 2026. The post emphasizes proactive relationship-building, leveraging AI tools for outreach efficiency, preparing for interviews before they arrive, exploring non-traditional career paths, and overcoming fear to take action. The caption reinforces that this is a starter template for navigating what can feel like a daunting process, drawing from personal experience in tech and consulting recruiting.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Insight 1:** Networking should begin as early as freshman year — waiting until you need referrals means you're already behind.
- **Insight 2:** AI tools (LLMs, extensions, CRMs like Dex) can automate the most tedious parts of cold outreach and follow-ups, saving days of manual work.
- **Insight 3:** Interview preparation should start before you have an interview scheduled — finding mock interview partners early is a game-changer.
- **Insight 4:** Don't default to traditional career paths (IB, consulting) just because they're what you hear about most — research broadly to discover roles you didn't know existed.
- **Insight 5:** Taking action before you feel ready is essential — the confidence and results follow from doing, not waiting.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

- **Slide 1:** Cover slide introducing the premise — "If I had to recruit again in 2026... this is everything I'd do." Establishes credibility: a senior who landed tech + startup offers.

- **Slide 2: Start Networking Way Earlier.** This is how you get advice you won't hear anywhere else. If she'd started freshman year, she wouldn't have scrambled later for referrals or advice. Even without knowing your exact career direction, building relationships early pays off when the time comes.

- **Slide 3: Use AI for Cold Outreach & Follow-Ups.** You can automate scheduling Google Calendar invites, follow-up emails, and cold outreach emails using AI extensions and LLMs. Recommended tools include Dex by ThirdLayer, ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity's Comet. This would have saved her days of manual work.

- **Slide 4: Prep for Interviews Before Getting One.** Don't wait until you land an interview to start preparing. Finding mock interview partners was one of the best things she did — she'd only change doing it even more in advance.

- **Slide 5: Explore Different Roles.** Don't choose something just because you think you're supposed to. You'll mostly hear about traditional paths (IB, consulting), but without researching what's out there, you won't know what you're missing. Do your research.

- **Slide 6: Everything You Want Is on the Other Side of Fear.** Take bets on yourself. That message to a leader you've been putting off, the negotiation you keep delaying, the startup you're eyeing but don't think you're cut out for — start taking action before you're ready. The rest always comes naturally.
