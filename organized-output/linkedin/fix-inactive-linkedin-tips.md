# 20 Ways to Fix Your Inactive LinkedIn

**Topic/Category:** Career Development / LinkedIn Growth
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To provide actionable tips for reviving a dormant LinkedIn profile through profile optimization and strategic networking, ultimately helping users attract recruiters and create career opportunities.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel post by Nishant Chahar (EF Fall'25, Building in Stealth, Ex-Algoprep) delivers a practical, no-fluff guide to transforming an inactive LinkedIn profile into an opportunity-generating machine. The post is split into two core pillars — Profile Optimisation and Networking & Outreach — offering roughly 20 specific, immediately actionable tips. The caption reinforces that most profiles stay inactive not out of laziness but from a lack of knowing what works, and positions this carousel as the fix.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Your profile is your landing page:** Treat it like a product — professional headshot, custom banner, keyword-rich headline, and impact-oriented experience bullets are non-negotiable.
- **Keywords drive discoverability:** Adding 3–5 keywords from job descriptions to your headline helps recruiters find you through LinkedIn's search algorithm.
- **Networking is active, not passive:** Sending personalized connection requests, following up within 24–48 hours, and leading with value are the keys to building a meaningful network.
- **500+ connections is the threshold:** Reaching 500+ connections quickly by targeting professionals with shared commonalities boosts visibility and social proof.
- **Engagement compounds:** Supporting others' posts with insightful comments before sending connection requests warms up the relationship and increases acceptance rates.

### Slide 2: Profile Optimisation
1. Use a friendly, professional headshot for your profile photo.
2. Remove the green "Open to Work" banner to avoid any bias.
3. Design a custom banner with your name, role, and email in Canva.
4. Use a standard job title in your headline (don't try to be clever).
5. Add 3–5 keywords from job descriptions to your headline.
6. Include your personality and what you enjoy doing outside of work in your About section.
7. Make your Experience section bullets impact-oriented, not daily-responsibilities-oriented.
8. Upload a media file to at least one experience entry (presentation, project, or article).
9. Reorder your skills for relevance in the Skills section and ask for endorsements for the most relevant ones.
10. Get to 500+ connections ASAP by targeting professionals who share commonalities with you.

### Slide 3: Networking and Outreach
1. Send connection requests to 10 former colleagues today.
2. Connect with 5–10 professionals in your target city.
3. Use the search string "[Your City] + [Your Role]" and connect with 5 people from the results.
4. Add personalized notes when reaching out to "high leverage" professionals who can open doors.
5. Mention a mutual connection or a shared interest in your connection request.
6. Message someone who accepted your request within 24–48 hours.
7. Send a "value-first" follow-up to a new connection.
8. Support your target's posts by adding insightful comments before sending a connection request.
9. Follow and engage with influential thought leaders in your industry to build visibility.
