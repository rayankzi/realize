# LinkedIn's Top 10 Marketing Skills You Need in 2026

**Topic/Category:** Marketing / Career Development
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To break down LinkedIn's official "Skills on the Rise 2026" list for marketing professionals, explaining each skill, recommending tools to learn it, and offering personal takes on why each one matters.

---

## Executive Summary

LinkedIn released its official Skills on the Rise list for 2026 targeting marketing professionals, and creator @marketingcareerwithpuja (Puja) broke down all 10 skills in an accessible carousel format. The biggest surprise: Prompt Engineering claimed the #1 spot — a skill many marketers had dismissed as "a tech person thing." The list spans a mix of AI-forward capabilities (prompt engineering, LLM optimisation), analytical competencies (performance analysis, data-driven decision making), and timeless marketing fundamentals (creative thinking, collaboration, market research). Each skill comes with recommended tools and Puja's personal commentary grounded in real-world marketing experience.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Prompt Engineering is the #1 rising marketing skill** — LinkedIn built this list from real hiring data, job posts, and recruiter searches, signaling that AI fluency is now a core marketing competency, not a niche tech skill.
- **The list blends AI skills with human skills** — While AI-related skills (prompt engineering, LLM optimisation) feature prominently, human-centric skills like creative thinking, collaboration, and market research remain essential.
- **Data literacy is a recurring theme** — Performance analysis (#3), data-driven decision making (#6), and SEO (#9) all require comfort with numbers and evidence-based thinking.
- **Visual storytelling and creative thinking are non-negotiable** — In a content-saturated landscape, the ability to translate complex ideas into compelling visuals and generate genuinely different ideas separates strategic marketers from executors.
- **Event planning is rising, especially in the UAE/Dubai market** — Experience-led marketing and in-person activations are making a strong comeback as a differentiator in an increasingly digital world.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

**Slide 1 (Cover):** Hook — "Marketing skills you need in 2026 before the job market leaves you behind." Features Puja with bold typography.

**Slide 2 (Context):** Explains that LinkedIn released its official Skills on the Rise list. Emphasizes this is built from real hiring data, real job posts, and real recruiter searches — not a random list.

**Slide 3 (Overview):** Frames the list — 10 fastest growing marketing skills, with the #1 spot going to a skill most marketers ignored until recently.

**Slide 4 — #1 Prompt Engineering:** The ability to write precise, well-structured instructions for AI tools. Described as "the new copywriting." Tools: Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini. Take: Marketers who brief AI well will produce more, faster, and at higher quality.

**Slide 5 — #2 Visual Storytelling:** Translating complex ideas into visuals people understand in three seconds. Tools: Canva, Photoshop, InDesign, Lightroom, Premiere Pro, CapCut, Figma.

**Slide 6 — #3 Performance Analysis:** Measuring ROI, conversion rates, cost per acquisition, and campaign attribution. Tools: Google Analytics, Meta Ads Manager, Power BI, HubSpot, Semrush. Take: You don't need to be good at maths — start with GA4 and learn what the numbers mean in plain English.

**Slide 7 — #4 Collaboration:** Working across teams (designers, sales, product, leadership) without things falling apart. Tools: Monday.com, Loom, Asana, Notion, Slack. Take: Being easy to work with and following through is genuinely rare and builds reputation fast.

**Slide 8 — #5 Creative Thinking:** Generating genuinely unexpected approaches to familiar problems. Creativity is a practice with actual frameworks. Take: Best ideas come from boredom, observation, and reading widely — not from staring at a brief.

**Slide 9 — #6 Data-Driven Decision Making:** Using actual evidence instead of gut feeling. Testing ideas before scaling them. Tools: Google Data Studio, Semrush, Airtable. Take: Track what you expect vs. what happens with every post — this trains your instincts with evidence.

**Slide 10 — #7 LLM Optimisation:** Fine-tuning AI language models so output sounds like your brand, not generic. Maintaining consistent voice and getting outputs that need minimal editing. Tools: Claude Projects. Take: Set up a custom system prompt with brand voice, audience, tone, and things to avoid.

**Slide 11 — #8 Event Planning and Coordination:** Organising real-world moments — product launches, experiential activations, pop-ups. Tools: Canva (event materials), Pinterest, Trello, Google Workspace. Take: Especially relevant in UAE/Dubai where brands are investing heavily in experience-led marketing.

**Slide 12 — #9 Search Engine Optimisation:** Getting found on Google organically. A well-optimised article from two years ago can still bring traffic today. Tools: Google Search Console, Yoast SEO, Google Keyword Planner. Take: SEO is unglamorous but delivers the most sustainable audiences and best long-term results.

**Slide 13 — #10 Market Research:** Knowing your audience through surveys, social listening, competitor analysis, trend spotting, and customer interviews. Tools: Google Trends, Reddit. Take: Reddit is one of the most underused research tools — real people, real opinions, zero filter.

**Slide 14 (Outro/CTA):** Puja introduces herself, encourages followers to engage, and asks which of the 10 skills they're already working on and which they're about to start.
