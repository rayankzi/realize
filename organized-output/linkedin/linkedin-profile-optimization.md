**Topic/Category:** Career / LinkedIn Optimization
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To show how a Product Manager landed interviews at Meta, Coinbase, and Datadog without applying — entirely through LinkedIn profile optimization — and break down the exact strategies used.

## Executive Summary

Michael Yitayew, a Product Manager at Google, shares how he landed three interviews at major tech companies (Meta, Coinbase, and Datadog) without submitting a single application. The secret was a strategically optimized LinkedIn profile. The carousel walks through six key profile elements — from headshot and banner to the About section, Experience bullets, content strategy, and recommendations — that turned his profile into a recruiter magnet. The caption reinforces the value by offering a free 1-pager optimization guide.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Professional headshot + branded banner** are the first impression — keep the photo shoulders-and-face only, and use the banner to reinforce your personal brand.
- **Summarize your value in one sentence** at the top of your About section, then list your biggest wins as scannable bullet points (people don't read long paragraphs).
- **Show, don't tell** in your Experience section — quantify impact with specific numbers (backlog size, customer adoption, team size, designs finalized) rather than vague descriptions.
- **Share learnings, not announcements** — shift your content from "here's what I did" to "here's HOW I did it" to provide behind-the-scenes value that attracts engagement.
- **Collect targeted recommendations** from the roles you collaborate with most (e.g., UX Designers and Engineering Managers for a PM) to build social proof.
- **Consistency across all sections** creates a cohesive personal brand that signals professionalism and intentionality to recruiters scanning profiles.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

- **Slide 1 — Hook:** "Landed 3 interviews without applying — here's how I used LinkedIn to do it." Shows logos of Meta, Coinbase, and Datadog to establish credibility.
- **Slide 2 — Headshot:** Use a clear, professional headshot showing only shoulders and face. Pair it with a branded banner image to reinforce your profile's identity.
- **Slide 3 — Summarize (About Section):** Lead with a one-sentence value proposition ("I build the invisible infrastructure powering great user/developer experiences"), then list your top 5 wins as numbered bullet points. Include links to your content (YouTube podcast, newsletter).
- **Slide 4 — Show, Don't Tell (Experience Section):** Demonstrate impact through numbers — managed a 100+ item backlog, delivered 4 customer-facing features in Q1, influenced 12+ design documents, helped unlock a multi-billion dollar opportunity. Also shows startup experience: went from 0 to 1, onboarded 50+ users, ranked #2 on Product Hunt.
- **Slide 5 — Share Learnings (Content Strategy):** Instead of copying typical LinkedIn content, share the "behind the scenes" of your career milestones. Examples shown include posts like "Learnings from my first 3 months in a new PM role" (600 reactions) and "After interviewing 15 of the top product managers I know across tech..." (1,332 reactions, 704 comments).
- **Slide 6 — Recommendations:** Proactively ask former co-workers for LinkedIn recommendations. Focus on the roles you collaborate with most. Examples show recommendations from a Mobile UX Design Lead and a Sr. Software Development Manager at AWS.
