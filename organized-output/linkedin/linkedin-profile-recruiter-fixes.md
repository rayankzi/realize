# 5 LinkedIn Profile Fixes to Get Noticed by Recruiters Right Now

**Topic/Category:** Career & LinkedIn Optimization
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Provide five immediate, actionable steps job seekers can take to optimize their LinkedIn profile for recruiter visibility and credibility.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel delivers a concise checklist of five LinkedIn profile optimizations designed to boost recruiter visibility and professional appeal. The creator (@meglovesdata) walks through algorithmic tricks (like toggling Open to Work), strategic skill placement, headline formatting, and featured section usage — all aimed at making a profile rank higher in recruiter searches while projecting confidence rather than desperation.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Toggling your "Open to Work" setting off and on resets your visibility in LinkedIn's algorithm, pushing you back into recruiter search results as a recently active profile.
- **Insight 2:** Skill endorsements directly impact recruiter search rankings — strategically ordering your top 3 skills maximizes endorsement likelihood on the ones that matter most.
- **Insight 3:** Language like "actively seeking" or "aspiring" and the public green Open to Work banner can signal desperation to recruiters and should be removed or hidden.
- **Insight 4:** A structured headline format ([Title] at [Company] | [Skills] | [Value Prop]) is both keyword-rich and scannable, making it easy for recruiters to quickly assess fit.
- **Insight 5:** The Featured section is underutilized real estate — pinning a portfolio link or an introductory post gives recruiters instant context beyond the resume.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

This is a tutorial-style reel structured as a numbered checklist:

1. **Reset Open to Work settings** — Turn the Open to Work toggle ON, wait 24 hours, then turn it OFF. This tricks the algorithm into treating your profile as freshly active.
2. **Add skills strategically** — Add 3–5 high-demand skills pulled from job descriptions for your target role. Reorder so your top 3 most relevant skills appear first to attract endorsements.
3. **Remove desperate signals** — Delete phrases like "actively seeking opportunities" or "aspiring" from your headline. Hide or remove the green Open to Work banner (recruiter-only visibility is acceptable).
4. **Update your headline** — Use the format: `[Title] at [Company] | [2-3 technical skills] | [Unique value proposition]`.
5. **Add to your Featured section** — Pin a link to your project portfolio or a self-introductory LinkedIn post to give recruiters immediate context about your work.
