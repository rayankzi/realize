# 20 Ways to Fix Your Inactive LinkedIn

**Topic/Category:** LinkedIn / Career Development / Networking
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Provide a comprehensive, actionable checklist of 20 tactics to revive a dormant LinkedIn profile — covering profile optimization and networking/outreach strategies.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel by Nishant Chahar (EF Fall'25, ex-Algoprep) delivers a practical 20-point playbook for transforming a stale LinkedIn presence into an opportunity-generating machine. The post is split into two core pillars: **Profile Optimisation** (making your profile discoverable and compelling) and **Networking and Outreach** (building genuine connections that lead to opportunities). The caption reinforces that most profiles stay inactive not from laziness but from a lack of knowledge about what actually works.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Profile completeness matters more than activity alone** — recruiters search by keywords, headlines, and skills sections, so optimizing these is the first lever to pull.
- **Impact over responsibilities** — experience bullets should be results-oriented, not duty-oriented.
- **Networking is a proactive, structured activity** — the post provides specific numbers (10 former colleagues, 5-10 local professionals, 500+ connections) to make outreach tangible.
- **Value-first engagement** — comment on posts, send personalized notes, and follow up with value before asking for anything.
- **Consistency compounds** — the tips emphasize daily/weekly habits (messaging within 24-48 hours, regular commenting) rather than one-time fixes.

### Slide 1: Cover — "20 Ways to Fix Your Inactive LinkedIn"

### Slide 2: Profile Optimisation (10 Tips)

1. Use a friendly, professional headshot for your profile photo.
2. Remove the green "Open to Work" banner to avoid any bias.
3. Design a custom banner with your name, role, and email in Canva.
4. Use a standard job title in your headline (don't try to be clever).
5. Add 3-5 keywords from job descriptions to your headline.
6. Include your personality and what you enjoy doing outside of work in your About section.
7. Make your Experience section bullets impact-oriented, not daily-responsibilities-oriented.
8. Upload a media file to at least one experience entry (presentation, project, or article).
9. Reorder your skills for relevance in the Skills section and ask for endorsements for the most relevant ones.
10. Get to 500+ connections ASAP by targeting professionals who share commonalities with you.

### Slide 3: Networking and Outreach (10 Tips)

1. Send connection requests to 10 former colleagues today.
2. Connect with 5-10 professionals in your target city.
3. Use the search string "[Your City] + [Your Role]" and connect with 5 people from the results.
4. Add personalized notes when reaching out to "high leverage" professionals who can open doors.
5. Mention a mutual connection or a shared interest in your connection request.
6. Message someone who accepted your request within 24-48 hours.
7. Send a "value-first" follow-up to a new connection.
8. Support your target's posts by adding insightful comments before sending a connection request.
9. Follow and engage with influential thought leaders in your industry to build visibility.
10. *(Implied from caption)* Build meaningful connections consistently over time.
