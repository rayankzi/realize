# How a 15-Minute Coffee Chat Turned Into a 2-Hour Conversation and a Job Referral

**Topic/Category:** Career Networking / Coffee Chat Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** To break down the exact tactics a non-traditional applicant used during a coffee chat that impressed the creator so much they extended the call to two hours and gave her a job referral.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel shares the story of a coffee chat that was supposed to last 15 minutes but extended to two hours — and ended with a job referral. The creator, a product manager who has pivoted across AR research, ed tech, and creator tools, highlights three specific behaviors the person used to stand out: showing genuine agency and curiosity from the very first question, asking deeply researched questions only the creator could answer, and framing her own situation so clearly that it was easy for the creator to give tailored advice. The key message is that even with a non-traditional background, thoughtfulness and preparation can open doors.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Replace generic small talk with signals of agency. Instead of "I'm good, how are you?" she said she had just finished a Claude Code tutorial and was excited to try it — immediately signaling curiosity, proactiveness, and energy.
- **Insight 2:** Do your homework and ask questions only that person can answer. She read the creator's Substack post about pivoting between PM roles and asked a specific question rooted in that content: "How do you decide when to diversify your experience versus deepen your expertise?"
- **Insight 3:** Make it easy for people to help you. Don't ask vague questions that require a bunch of clarifying follow-ups. She explained her specific dilemma (choosing between product marketing and product management), drew parallels and differences to the creator's path, and synthesized the context needed for targeted advice.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning (The Hook):** The creator opens with a bold statement — she hung up after a two-hour coffee chat, immediately referred the person, even though the candidate came from a non-traditional background. This sets up a curiosity gap: what did this person do differently?

**Middle (The Three Tactics):**

1. **Show agency and curiosity from the start.** When asked "How are you?", most people give a throwaway answer. This person used it as an opportunity to demonstrate she was actively learning (Claude Code tutorial), creating an immediate impression of being self-driven and current with industry tools. The creator felt both relieved (wouldn't have to carry the conversation) and invested in her success.

2. **Ask researched, unique questions.** She had clearly read the creator's Substack post about pivoting between different PM domains — AR research, healthcare, ed tech, content creation tools. Her question about diversifying vs. deepening expertise was specific to the creator's unique career trajectory, making the conversation feel valuable rather than generic.

3. **Help them help you.** Beyond asking a good question, she contextualized her own dilemma, explaining the parallels and differences between her situation and the creator's. This removed the burden of guessing and made it frictionless for the creator to provide useful, targeted advice.

**End (The Result):** The combination of these behaviors turned a 15-minute coffee chat into a 2-hour conversation and ultimately earned her a job referral.
