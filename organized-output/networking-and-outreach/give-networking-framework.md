# The GIVE Framework: Stop Cold DMing and Activate Your Warm Network

**Topic/Category:** Career, Networking, Job Search
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To introduce the GIVE framework — a simple 4-step method for leveraging your existing warm network instead of relying on low-reply-rate cold outreach during a job search.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel argues that most job seekers waste time grinding through cold outreach when they already have a warm network of people who know their name — classmates, ex-colleagues, people in their city, and mutual connections. The creator introduces the "GIVE" framework (Get specific, Initiate with value, Visit consistently, Exit with a next step) as an embarrassingly simple 4-step system to activate these warm contacts. The core promise: pick just 15 people, commit to 90 days, and start with whoever is most likely to respond — not the most impressive person on your list.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Cold outreach reply rates are embarrassingly low.** Activating your warm network is the highest-ROI move in a job search, yet nobody talks about it.
- **Your warm network already exists.** Uni/school classmates, ex-colleagues, people in your city (especially as an expat), and mutual connections are all warmer than strangers — and are being ignored.
- **Quality over quantity.** Pick 15 specific people, not 500. Target people 2-3 steps ahead of you, in roles you want, or in rooms you're not in yet.
- **Lead with value, not asks.** Reference specific shared context or their recent work. No generic "hope you're well" messages.
- **Consistency beats intensity.** Engage with their content, share relevant things, reference past conversations — people notice sustained attention.
- **Every conversation needs a next step.** Without one, you're forgotten by tomorrow.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

**Slide 1 — Hook:** "Do this before you send another Cold DM" — a photo of the creator at a Paris cafe with the subtitle "Exposing the Networking Framework no one talks about."

**Slide 2 — The Problem:** Lists four categories of warm contacts most people ignore: uni/school classmates, ex-colleagues, people in your city (especially expats), and mutual connections. Punchline: "All warmer than a stranger. All ignored."

**Slide 3 — The Insight:** Cold outreach reply rates are embarrassing. Activating your warm network is the highest ROI move in your job search. Nobody talks about it.

**Slide 4 — The Framework Introduction:** The GIVE framework mapped into 4 steps:
1. **G — Get specific**
2. **I — Initiate with value**
3. **V — Visit consistently**
4. **E — Exit with a next step**

**Slide 5 — G + I Deep Dive:**
- **Get specific:** Pick 15 people (not 500). People 2-3 steps ahead of you, in roles you want, in rooms you're not in yet. Write the list today.
- **Initiate with value:** Not "Hope you're well, would love to catch up." Instead: "Saw your post on XYZ. Reminded me of when we worked on Y together. Here's what I've been thinking about it." Warm context, specific reference, no ask yet.

**Slide 6 — V + E Deep Dive:**
- **Visit consistently:** Comment on their content. Share something relevant to them. Reference something they said 3 months ago. Start paying attention — they'll feel it.
- **Exit with a next step:** Every conversation needs somewhere to go. Examples: "I'll send you that," "Let's connect when you're in Dubai," "Would love your take when you have 10 mins." No next step means you're forgotten by tomorrow.

**Slide 7 — The Challenge:** 15 people. 90 days. Start with whoever would reply the fastest — not the most impressive person on the list. "One reply and you'll wish you'd done this sooner!!"
