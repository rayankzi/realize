# Who to Network With for Engineering Internships (Ranked)

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Networking Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Provide engineering students with a prioritized ranking of who to reach out to when networking for summer internships, cutting through generic "just network" advice.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel targets engineering students still searching for summer internships and offers a concrete, ranked framework for who to network with — moving beyond vague "just network" advice. The creator ranks contacts from least to most effective: new employees (least influence), executives (too busy), middle management (the hiring decision-makers), and at the top, middle managers or executives who share a specific background with you. The caption reinforces engagement by polling viewers on how they landed their own internships.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Not all networking contacts are equal — prioritize based on their influence over hiring decisions, not their seniority alone.
- **Insight 2:** Middle management (managers and directors) is the gold standard for networking because they review applicants, conduct interviews, and make hiring decisions. You may even end up working directly for them.
- **Insight 3:** The highest-priority targets are middle managers or executives with whom you share a specific background (same university, same student org, etc.) — the more specific the overlap, the stronger the connection.
- **Insight 4:** New employees and C-suite executives are lower priority — new hires lack hiring influence, and executives are too busy and removed from the hiring process.
- **Insight 5:** The creator personally landed a SpaceX internship using this middle-management networking strategy, lending credibility to the approach.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning (Hook):** The creator directly addresses the pain point — engineering students tired of being told to "just network" — and promises a ranked, actionable list of who to actually reach out to.

**Middle (Core Ranking):**

1. **Lowest Priority — New Employees:** People new to the company lack influence and are still building their own credibility internally. They're unlikely to sway hiring decisions.
2. **Priority #3 — Executives (CEOs, VPs, Presidents):** While they hold power, they have too much going on to be directly involved in hiring. Your message may never get seen.
3. **Priority #2 (Gold Standard) — Middle Management (Managers & Directors):** These are the people reviewing applications, interviewing candidates, and making offers. If you land a role, you may report directly to them. The creator notes this is how they landed their SpaceX internship.
4. **Priority #1 — Shared Background Contacts in Groups 2 & 3:** The top-tier networking targets are middle managers or executives who share a background with you. Same university is good; same university + same student org is even better. Specificity strengthens the connection.

**End (Conclusion):** The ranking naturally concludes at #1, with the creator emphasizing the power of shared, specific backgrounds as the ultimate networking advantage.
