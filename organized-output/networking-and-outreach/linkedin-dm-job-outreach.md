# The LinkedIn DM That Got Me My First Job

**Topic/Category:** Job Search Strategy / Networking
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** To teach students and job seekers how to write concise, effective LinkedIn DMs and cold outreach messages that actually get responses.

---

## Executive Summary

The creator shares a direct, experience-backed lesson on LinkedIn outreach: keep your DMs and emails extremely short. They explain that most professionals have short attention spans and will stop reading long, detailed messages by the second line. The creator landed their first role by sending a brief LinkedIn DM that led to a referral and an interview, and they encourage viewers to follow the same approach — two to three lines max. The caption adds a personal, empathetic note acknowledging how difficult the job search process is.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Brevity wins:** Professionals receiving cold outreach have short attention spans. Long emails with your full background and motivations will lose them by line two.
- **Short DMs get responses:** Keep your LinkedIn DM or email to two or three lines. This dramatically increases your chances of getting a reply.
- **Referrals come from outreach:** The creator got their first job by DMing someone on LinkedIn who then referred them, leading to an interview. Cold outreach works when done right.
- **Don't over-explain:** You don't need to include your entire resume, every accomplishment, or a detailed explanation of why you want the role. Just be concise and direct.

- **Beginning (Hook):** The creator directly addresses students who are DMing people on LinkedIn asking for coffee chats, immediately calling out a common mistake — signaling this is targeted, practical advice.
- **Middle (Core Argument):** They explain that recipients have the same short attention span as everyone else. Long messages with background details, accomplishments, and role justifications cause readers to check out by the second line. The creator empathizes: "I'm you, you are me."
- **End (Proof & CTA):** They share that their own first role came from a LinkedIn DM that resulted in a referral and interview. They reference showing "the exact DM that got me my job" and clarify you don't need to copy it verbatim — just keep it to two or three lines.
