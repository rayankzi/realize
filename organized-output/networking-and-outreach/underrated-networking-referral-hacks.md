# 3 Underrated Networking Hacks That Actually Get Referrals

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Networking Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Share three practical, non-obvious networking strategies that help early-career professionals land referrals and build meaningful connections.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel presents three underutilized networking tactics aimed at new grads and early-career professionals: a LinkedIn keyword filtering hack to find hyper-relevant connections, leveraging case competitions and hackathons as direct pipelines to recruiters, and conducting quarterly audits of your existing network to capitalize on career transitions. The creator backs each tip with personal examples, emphasizing that specificity and follow-through are what separate effective networking from cold outreach that gets ignored.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** LinkedIn's advanced filters (school, company, location, keywords) can be stacked to find people with extremely specific shared backgrounds — the more niche the overlap, the higher the response rate and the richer the conversation.
- **Insight 2:** Case competitions and hackathons are an underrated recruiting bypass — companies send scouts, winners get fast-tracked, and even non-winners can convert in-person interactions into warm leads through follow-ups.
- **Insight 3:** Your existing network is a renewable resource. Checking in every quarter on where mentors and close connections have moved creates natural, low-effort touchpoints that often yield fresh career insights and referral opportunities.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning — LinkedIn Keywords + "Lowkey Stalking"**
The creator introduces a LinkedIn search hack: stack multiple filters (school, current company, previous company, location) and add niche keyword searches (e.g., "Theatre," "Economics") to surface people with highly specific shared backgrounds. They share a real example they used — searching for someone who went to Western/Queens, currently works at Google, previously at BCG, based in NYC, with "Theatre" as a keyword. The more specific the filters, the more likely you'll get a response and a genuinely engaging conversation that can evolve into mentorship. Bonus tip: scroll LinkedIn like TikTok — study people's posts, likes, mutual connections, and followed companies to prepare for coffee chats.

**Middle — Case Competitions + Hackathons**
The second strategy reframes competitions as networking and recruiting tools, not just resume boosters. Companies send recruiters to scout talent at these events. Winners often receive automatic interviews or exclusive networking access. The key move for non-winners: talk to company reps during the event and always send follow-up messages afterward. In-person contact dramatically increases response rates compared to cold outreach.

**End — Quarterly Connections Audit**
Described as the creator's "best kept secret," this tactic is simple: every three months, review where your close connections and mentors have moved. Career transitions (e.g., leaving consulting for a PM role at Microsoft, or banking for a YC startup) create natural windows to reconnect — people in transition have more availability and fresh perspectives. The creator notes this practice also helped them map career progressions and plan their own next moves.
