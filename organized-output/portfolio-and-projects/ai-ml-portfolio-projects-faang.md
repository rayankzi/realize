## Executive Summary

This carousel presents a comprehensive collection of industry-grade AI/ML project ideas designed to replace weak, tutorial-based portfolio projects with ones that actually impress FAANG/MAANG recruiters and interviewers. The projects are grouped into five categories by role and specialization — Core ML, NLP/LLM, Computer Vision, Data Engineering + ML, and Advanced/Differentiator projects — so candidates can pick what fits their career path. Each project is meant to solve a real problem, use industry practices, and be something interviewers actually want to discuss.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **GPA doesn't kill ML resumes — weak projects do.** The post's core thesis is that real-world, problem-solving projects matter far more than academic credentials.
- **Projects should be "1%-level"** — meaning they go beyond toy datasets and tutorials, demonstrating production-grade thinking.
- **Each project should solve a real business problem** and follow industry-standard ML practices (monitoring, drift detection, proper evaluation metrics).
- **Role-based organization** lets candidates focus on projects relevant to their target position (applied ML, NLP, CV, data engineering, or generalist/advanced).
- **Interviewers care about WHY you built it and HOW you thought through it**, not just that you followed a tutorial.
