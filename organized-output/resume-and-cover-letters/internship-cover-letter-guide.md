# How to Write the Best Cover Letter for Internships or Jobs as a Student

**Topic/Category:** Career Tips / Job Search
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To teach college, graduate, and high school students how to write a compelling cover letter by breaking it into three clear sections — opening, experience, and role connection — with templates and examples.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel provides a complete, section-by-section framework for writing a cover letter tailored to internships, fellowships, scholarships, and early career roles. The core message is that a cover letter should communicate what a resume cannot: critical thinking, collaboration, leadership, and communication skills. Each section includes actionable advice, a "start by explaining" checklist, and a fill-in-the-blank template paragraph that students can adapt to their own experience. A full combined example (for a finance/Treasury Analyst role) and a link to a Google Doc with additional examples are also provided.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Insight 1:** A cover letter's purpose is to showcase soft skills and personal qualities (critical thinking, collaboration, leadership, communication) that a resume's bullet points cannot convey.
- **Insight 2:** Follow a "show, don't tell" approach — describe specific experiences that demonstrate traits rather than simply listing them.
- **Insight 3:** The cover letter should be structured in three sections: (1) an opening that explains your interest and curiosity, (2) a body that details your experience and growth, and (3) a closing that connects your background directly to the role you're applying for.
- **Insight 4:** Generic openings like "I am excited to apply for this internship" are weak — a stronger opening ties your academic focus and genuine curiosity to the specific role.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

**Section 1 — Strong Opening Template:**

> My name [name]. As a student studying [major], I've become increasingly interested in seeing how the ideas I learn in class are applied in real organizations [Expand on what you want to learn]. The [internship title] role stood out to me because it offers the opportunity to contribute while continuing to learn from people working in this field +[add what you want to be exposed to]

**Section 2 — Experience and Growth Template:**

> In my current role working on [project or initiative], I initially supported [task or responsibility], but over time assumed greater ownership in organizing information and structuring the work more effectively. As I became more familiar with the process, I focused on improving coordination and maintaining clarity around priorities so the team could progress efficiently.
>
> In addition to this role, I worked with [company, organization, or student group], supporting efforts related to [area or responsibility]. Across these experiences, my responsibilities expanded from contributing to tasks to organizing workflows, coordinating across teams, and maintaining consistent progress. These experiences strengthened my understanding of how initiative, accountability, and execution support effective leadership within a team.

**Section 3 — Connecting to the Role Template:**

> Additionally, I have experience with [experience 2 / company or organization], where I supported [initiative or project]. At one point, [brief real moment — example: a deadline shifted, new information came in, or priorities changed], and I helped reorganize materials and updates so the work could continue moving forward without delays. Being part of moments like that showed me how quickly priorities can shift and how important it is to stay attentive to details while keeping the larger objective in mind. Experiences like this are part of what draws me to the [role title] position at [Company Name], where that same level of awareness and follow-through helps ensure projects continue progressing smoothly.

**Full Combined Example (Finance / Treasury Analyst):**

> Dear Hiring Manager,
>
> As a student studying finance, I have become increasingly interested in understanding how financial systems operate behind the scenes and how organizations manage complex transactions at scale. The Treasury Analyst role stood out to me because it offers the opportunity to see how payment infrastructure, client relationships, and financial operations come together in practice.
>
> In my current role supporting payment operations, I initially assisted with reviewing transaction activity and identifying discrepancies within the payment system. As I became more familiar with the process, I focused on improving coordination between internal teams so that issues could be addressed efficiently and updates remained clear.
