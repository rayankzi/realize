# Google Recruiter Leaked This AI Resume Hack Using Jobsuit.ai

**Topic/Category:** Job Search / AI Resume Tools
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** To demonstrate how jobsuit.ai can automatically tailor your resume to any job description by extracting and inserting missing keywords — presented as an insider hack allegedly leaked by a Google recruiter.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel showcases jobsuit.ai, a free AI resume builder that automatically matches your resume to a specific job posting. The creator walks through a three-step workflow: copy a job description from LinkedIn, paste it into jobsuit.ai, and let the tool identify missing keywords from the posting — then automatically insert them into your existing resume bullets. The final result is a fully tailored, ATS-optimized resume that can be reviewed and accepted with one click. The caption frames this as a secret leaked by a Google recruiter, adding urgency and intrigue.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Keyword matching is critical for ATS:** The tool identifies keywords from job descriptions (e.g., "Agile development," "Cloud-based processing") that are missing from your resume and suggests where to naturally insert them.
- **AI modifies bullets contextually, not blindly:** Rather than just appending keywords, the tool rewrites resume bullet points to organically incorporate the missing terms while preserving the original meaning and metrics.
- **The tool provides reasoning for each change:** Each keyword modification comes with a "Reasoning" explanation for why the change was made, allowing users to accept or reject edits individually.
- **Built-in AI chat assistant:** Jobsuit.ai includes a chat sidebar that can help users add new work experiences, rewrite sections, and provide feedback on the resume.
- **One-click accept/reject workflow:** Users can review all suggested changes and accept or decline them individually or in bulk before finalizing the resume.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. **Step 1 — Find and copy a job description:** The video opens on a LinkedIn job listing for an "Entry Level Software Developer" position at SkillStorm (via Lensa), posted in New York, NY. On-screen text instructs: "Copy the job description." The full job posting is visible including requirements, responsibilities, and company info.

2. **Step 2 — Paste into jobsuit.ai:** The creator navigates to jobsuit.ai's "Add New Job" page. An "Auto-Format" tab is shown, with a text area labeled "Job Posting Text" where users paste the complete job description. On-screen text reads: "paste it in jobsuit.ai." The job description text is pasted and the "Format Job Details" button is clicked.

3. **Step 3 — Review keyword matching results:** The tool's "Keyword Matching" feature activates, showing a progress bar of "Keywords Integrated." It displays 21 active keyword modifications, 5 already matched, and 0 rejected. Each keyword (e.g., "Agile development," "Cloud-based processing") shows the original bullet, the modified bullet, and the reasoning behind the change.

4. **Step 4 — Accept changes and view final resume:** The creator clicks "Accept Revision" on modifications. The right sidebar shows an AI chat assistant offering to help improve specific sections. The final frame reveals the completed, tailored resume (using a placeholder name "Elon Musk") with a Professional Summary, Work Experience at Google and Microsoft, an Amazon internship, Education, and Skills sections — all with keywords seamlessly integrated. Buttons for "Accept Changes," "Show Changes," and "Decline Changes" are visible at the top.
