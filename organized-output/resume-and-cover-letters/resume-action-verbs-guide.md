# 80 Action Verbs for a Stronger Resume

**Topic/Category:** Career / Resume Writing
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To provide job seekers with 80 powerful action verbs organized by skill category, replacing weak or generic phrasing on resumes with impactful language that highlights qualifications.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel from Resume Genius presents 80 strong action verbs categorized across 8 professional skill areas to help job seekers upgrade their resume language. The post emphasizes that swapping out generic, vague phrases for specific action verbs makes resumes stand out by more clearly highlighting skills and achievements. Each slide focuses on a different competency area, offering 10 curated verbs per category that can immediately be dropped into resume bullet points.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Action verbs replace passive language:** Starting bullet points with strong verbs like "Orchestrated" or "Refactored" is far more compelling than "Responsible for" or "Helped with."
- **Category-specific verbs matter:** Different roles call for different verb families — a leadership role benefits from "Directed" and "Delegated," while a technical role is better served by "Programmed" and "Configured."
- **80 verbs across 8 categories:** The post provides a comprehensive but manageable reference covering creativity, leadership, technical skills, teamwork, research, accomplishments, organization, and sales.
- **Industry-specific verb selection is key:** The final slide's key takeaways advise using industry-specific action verbs that match the role (e.g., leadership words for management, technical verbs for IT).
