## Executive Summary

A college junior, feeling lost about post-graduation plans, decides to stop waiting for direction and start creating it. The experiment: cold-reach out to 100 people they admire — journalists, investors, founders, creators — with no motive other than to learn how they think and built their careers. Progress is being tracked in Notion, and after the first five outreach attempts, one person has responded. The caption frames this as Part 1 of an ongoing series.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Proactive beats passive:** Instead of waiting for clarity or opportunity, the creator chose to manufacture it by initiating conversations with people they look up to.
- **No-ask networking works:** The entire premise is reaching out without requesting a job, referral, or favor — just genuine curiosity about how these people built their paths.
- **Tracking creates accountability:** Using Notion to log every outreach attempt turns a vague goal into a measurable challenge with visible progress.
- **Realistic expectations matter:** A 1-out-of-5 response rate so far is shared honestly, setting authentic expectations for viewers who might try the same approach.
- **The series format builds momentum:** Framing this as "Part 1" creates a narrative arc that keeps both the creator and audience invested in the outcome.
