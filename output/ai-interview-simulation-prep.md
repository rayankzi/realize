# How to Use AI to Actually Ace Your Next Interview

**Topic/Category:** Career / Interview Prep
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach viewers a practical, step-by-step method for using AI tools (like Claude or Copilot) to simulate real interviews instead of just passively researching — shifting from "prepared" to genuinely comfortable and confident.

---

## Executive Summary

Most people use AI for interview prep the same way they use Google — reading company info and rehearsing answers in their head. This reel argues that's ineffective. The real unlock is using AI as a live interview simulator: feeding it your resume and job description, turning on voice mode, and practicing answering questions out loud in real-time. Combined with self-recording and iterative feedback loops, this method transforms interview prep from passive research into active performance training.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Passive research (reading about the company, mentally rehearsing answers) doesn't translate to interview performance. You need to practice *speaking* your answers out loud under pressure.
- **Insight 2:** AI tools like Claude and Copilot can be turned into realistic interview simulators by providing your resume + job description and asking them to act as your interviewer in voice mode.
- **Insight 3:** The feedback loop is critical — get AI feedback after each answer (what went well, what to improve, how to be more specific), then layer in self-observation by recording yourself.
- **Insight 4:** The goal isn't to feel "prepared" — it's to feel *comfortable*. Repeated simulation makes the actual interview feel familiar rather than novel, which is what drives real confidence.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Step 1: Set Up Your AI Interviewer**
- Drop your resume and the job description into Claude or Copilot.
- Prompt it: *"Act as my interviewer for this role and ask me questions live."*
- Turn on voice mode so you're speaking, not typing.

**Step 2: Simulate the Interview in Real-Time**
- Answer questions out loud as they come — no pausing, no overthinking.
- After each answer, ask for structured feedback:
  - What did I do well?
  - What can I improve?
  - How can I make this more specific?
- Treat it like a real interview, not casual practice.

**Step 3: Record Yourself**
- Set up a camera and record your responses.
- Watch it back and evaluate:
  - Are you rambling?
  - Are you confident or hesitant?
  - What's your body language like?
  - Are you fidgeting or distracted?

**Step 4: Combine Feedback and Iterate**
- Merge the AI's feedback with your own self-observations.
- Practice again. Repeat multiple times.
- The shift happens when the interview stops feeling new and starts feeling familiar.
