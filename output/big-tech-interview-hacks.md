# Easy Hacks to Land Big Tech Interviews (That Most People Skip)

**Topic/Category:** Career / Tech Internships
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Share actionable, underutilized strategies that helped the creator land interviews at top tech companies like Google, Meta, Apple, Microsoft, TikTok, Salesforce, Intuit, and Capital One — going beyond just submitting applications online.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel breaks down seven concrete tactics for getting interviews at big tech companies, emphasizing that landing the interview is the hardest part of the process. Rather than relying solely on cold applications, the creator shares strategies centered on networking, timing, keyword optimization, and leveraging programs and communities that create direct pipelines to recruiters. The overall message: these hacks aren't complicated, but they require initiative beyond "apply and hope."

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Insight 1:** Getting the interview is the hardest part — once you're in the pipeline, your odds improve dramatically. Focus your energy on getting past the resume screen.
- **Insight 2:** Cold applications are the least effective method. Direct recruiter contact, referrals, and pipeline programs consistently outperform them.
- **Insight 3:** Timing matters enormously — applying within the first hour a role drops significantly increases visibility.
- **Insight 4:** Second-degree connections (friends of friends) are statistically more effective for job referrals than first-degree connections.
- **Insight 5:** Pattern-matching what has already worked for people at your school removes guesswork and increases your chances.
- **Insight 6:** Company summits, hackathons, and exploratory programs are a "quiet way in" that can auto-pipeline you into interviews.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

**Slide 1 — Cover:** "Easy hacks I used to get big tech interviews (idk why more ppl aren't doing these)" — features logos of Google, Meta, Microsoft, Apple, TikTok, Salesforce, Intuit, and Capital One.

**Slide 2 — The Truth:** Getting an interview is THE HARDEST PART. Once you're in, your chances go way up. These aren't complicated — they just require you to do more than just apply and hope.

**Slide 3 — Find Your University Recruiter on LinkedIn:** Big tech companies assign recruiters to cover specific schools. Search LinkedIn for terms like "university recruiter," "early career," or "campus recruiting" alongside the company name. Filter by People, Actively Hiring, and 1st-degree connections. Look for someone with many mutual connections from your school — that's usually your assigned recruiter. Getting your resume in front of them beats a cold apply every time.

**Slide 4 — Apply Within the First Hour It Drops:** Early applicants have a much higher chance of being seen. Track when companies usually post by looking at LinkedIn posts from employees and old Reddit threads. Use the Wayback Machine or Google to find old job descriptions for the same role so you can prepare keywords in advance. Have your resume ready before the posting even goes live.

**Slide 5 — Apply to Company Summits and Exploratory Programs:** Programs like Google's LSLS Summit can auto-pipeline you into the SWE intern interview process — no normal application needed. Summits, hackathons, and exploratory programs are the best quiet way in. Programs to look up: Jane Street INSIGHT, Inside HRT, Salesforce Futureforce Tech Launchpad, EY Expedition.

**Slide 6 — Copy What Worked for People from Your School:** Find people from your school who landed the exact role you want. Look at what orgs they were in, what positions they held, and what their resume looked like. Then do the same things. No creativity needed — just pattern match what's already working.

**Slide 7 — Apply to Programs That Partner with Tech Companies:** Professional development programs like MLT have formal partnerships with big tech companies. Companies recruit heavily from these because candidates are already vetted. Being in a program like this gets you in front of recruiters at Apple, Google, and others in a way that a cold apply never will. You also get interview prep and community.

**Slide 8 — Put Keywords from the Job Description into Your Resume:** The more your resume matches the job description, the better your chances — especially with ATS filters. Pull the exact words they use and work them into your resume naturally. It takes 20 minutes but matters a lot.

**Slide 9 — Use Your Friend's Friends, Not Just Your Friends:** Research shows most jobs come through second-degree connections, not first. The creator got interviews at competitive roles because a friend connected them to someone already in the company, and that referral was basically required to get an interview.

**Slide 10 — Internship Job Boards Resource:** The creator compiled a list of the most updated job boards (categorized by Software Engineering, Product/Data/Analytics, Finance/Quant, Marketing/Design, etc.) so you can apply the second a role drops. Timing is EVERYTHING.
