# Skills You Need to Master as a Big Tech Product Manager

**Topic/Category:** Tech / Product Management / Career
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To break down the 8 core skills aspiring and current Product Managers need to master in order to succeed at big tech companies like FAANG and Microsoft.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel provides a comprehensive overview of the essential skill set required to thrive as a Product Manager in big tech. The creator — a Gen Z PM currently in corporate — walks through 8 distinct competency areas that define the day-to-day reality of the role, from product discovery and competitive research to stakeholder management and decision making. The caption reinforces that PM is a role best learned by doing, recommending productathons, side projects, and startup experience over pure theory. Each slide pairs a real-life photo (office, lunch, elevator selfie, city street) with an overlaid skill description and a clever mock UI notification that illustrates the concept.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Product Management is experiential:** The caption emphasizes that theory (product sense, execution metrics) only clicks once you're actually doing the job — learning from the outside is insufficient.
- **8 Core PM Skills identified:** Product Discovery, Project Execution, Competitive Research, Experimentation Design, Stakeholder Management, Product Decision Making, Product Design, and Managing Vertically & Horizontally.
- **Cross-functional collaboration is everywhere:** Nearly every skill involves working with engineering, design, data science, leadership, or XFN partners.
- **Decision making is the most important skill:** The post explicitly calls out Product Decision Making as "arguably the most important skill as a Product Manager."
- **Data-driven mindset is critical:** From A/B experiments to success metrics to competitive benchmarking, PMs must be comfortable defining and acting on data.
- **Breaking in requires hands-on experience:** Productathons, side projects, and smaller company/startup PM roles are recommended entry paths.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

- **Slide 1 — Introduction:** Hook slide featuring the creator at a laptop with headphones, text reads "...so you want to be a big tech Product Manager? here's some skills you'll need to master" with FAANG and Microsoft logos. A mock iMessage notification from "Big Tech" asks "How's your product sense & execution?"

- **Slide 2 — Product Discovery:** From user research to solution scoping, a PM defines the "what" and the "why" of a product. You get inputs from engineering, design, PM, business, customers, external partners, and more. It's your job to distill all these signals into a clear vision and winning roadmap. Mock UI shows a "UXR Session 1 — Customer" audio playback widget.

- **Slide 3 — Project Execution:** Building the project GANTT, planning product launches, working with product operations to ensure smooth releases. "Unblocking" engineering and XFN partners by finding the right answer or driving alignment on open questions. As a PM, you do whatever it takes to ensure the success of the product. Mock UI shows a Crashlytics-style dialog: "Is everything on track? Yes / No." Also features "free lunch!" with a photo of an office salad.

- **Slide 4 — Competitive Research:** Conducting extensive competitive benchmarking. Study and present insights on what other market players are doing, what's their strategy vs. ours, and where is our "right to win." Done both passively and actively for features/products you're working on. Mock notification from "Tech News" reads: "Did you see X company's newest launch?" Photo shows someone grabbing a Coke from a stocked fridge — a visual metaphor for comparing options.

- **Slide 5 — Experimentation Design:** Heavy collaboration with Data Science to define A/B experiments and product success metrics. How do you know if a product launch is successful or when to stop a release because it's failing? As a PM, you're both defining the metrics and making decisions based on experiment data. Mock speech bubble: "User churn is up post-launch, why?"

- **Slide 6 — Stakeholder Management:** You'll need top-notch collaboration skills and can't shy away from working with a LOT of stakeholders. Every group has their unique needs and perspectives. It's important as a PM to be an advocate for the product POV towards other stakeholders (i.e., what you think the right answer is). Mock Airdrop notification: "10 meeting invites tomorrow — Decline / Accept."

- **Slide 7 — Product Decision Making:** Arguably the most important skill as a Product Manager. Decisions range from evaluating trade-offs (e.g., "should we spend an extra 2 weeks and launch to an additional user cohort?") to "this button failed, what should we do next?" As a PM, you're making decisions on a daily basis, from small execution details to big strategic questions. If you're indecisive or scared of decision fatigue, product management will be a hard job.

- **Slide 8 — Product Design:** Close collaboration with designers to define the ideal product experience. What makes the experience delightful, where can it be improved, how do we design to achieve certain user actions or boost specific metrics (e.g., retention)? Mock error dialog: "How's the product experience? OK."

- **Slide 9 — Managing Vertically & Horizontally:** You have to influence leadership decisions through Product Reviews or Quarterly Planning (managing vertically). Simultaneously, you constantly work horizontally with other PMs and XFN partners to execute on projects. From resourcing (e.g., "can your Engineers work on this?") to prioritization ("let's deprioritize this project because of XYZ"). Mock UI shows a highway sign: "Q1 Launch? — Yes." Set against a busy city street at night.
