## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Lead with intention, not improvisation:** Setting context and sharing why you specifically wanted to speak with someone signals respect and prevents the conversation from feeling aimless.
- **Make it about them, not you:** People remember conversations where they felt heard, not pitched to. Asking open-ended questions about their journey is far more effective than talking about yourself.
- **Show strategic awareness:** Asking market-relevant questions (e.g., about hiring shifts or AI's impact) positions you as thoughtful and informed, not just another job-seeker.
- **Close with relationship, not transaction:** Ending with "I'd love to stay in touch" rather than "Do you have any openings?" builds the kind of ongoing connections that actually lead to opportunities.
- **Brevity about yourself is a strength:** A short, honest snapshot of where you're at helps people understand how to help you without making the conversation feel one-sided.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

This is an informational reel structured as a step-by-step guide:

1. **Set the Context & Intention** — Thank them for their time, share why you specifically wanted to speak with them, mention how you found them (referral, LinkedIn, content, etc.), and briefly outline what you hope to cover.

2. **Make It Mostly About Them** — Focus on their journey, decisions, transitions, and experiences. Ask thoughtful, open-ended questions like:
   - "What led you in this direction?"
   - "What shaped your career the most early on?"
   - "What's something people often misunderstand about your role or industry?"

3. **Briefly Share Your Context** — Offer a short, honest snapshot of where you're at. Keep it concise and authentic.

4. **Elevate With Market-Relevant Curiosity** — Ask questions that show awareness of the industry and the bigger picture:
   - "What hiring shifts are you noticing right now?"
   - "How is AI changing the way your team or firm operates?"

5. **Close With Next Steps** — End with intention, not a transaction:
   - "I'd love to stay in touch and keep you posted."
   - "If there's ever a way I can add value or support you, please keep me in mind."
