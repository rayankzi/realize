**Topic/Category:** Job Search / Career Strategy

## Executive Summary

The creator shares a cold email strategy they personally used to land a new job by emailing the head of recruiting directly. ## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Subject line is make-or-break:** It determines whether the email gets opened at all. Options range from straightforward ("Interested in XYZ role at Company") to bold and value-driven ("You're losing 10x on sales because of this mistake").
- **Brevity wins:** The entire email should be concise and on point — no long paragraphs or life stories.
- **Three-part body structure:** (1) One-line warm intro + why you're a fit + personal connection showing research, (2) 3–4 bullet points of most relevant experience, (3) specific roles you're interested in with links attached.
- **Always end with a call to action and attach your resume:** Make it easy for the recruiter to take the next step.
- **Cold emailing works in competitive markets:** The creator used this exact approach to land multiple interviews successfully.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning (Hook):** Opens with a personal success story — "I landed a new job by sending a cold email to the head of recruiting" — immediately establishing credibility and viewer interest.

**Middle (The Framework):**

1. **Craft the subject line** — Choose between a straightforward approach (e.g., "Interested in XYZ role at Company") or a creative, attention-grabbing one (e.g., "You're losing 10x on sales because of this mistake").
2. **Write Part 1 of the body** — A warm one-line introduction covering who you are, why you'd be a good fit for that specific company, and a personal connection (e.g., you tried their product or admire their culture). Show you did your research.
3. **Write Part 2 of the body** — Add 3–4 bullet points highlighting your most relevant work experience.
4. **Write Part 3 of the body** — List the specific job roles you're interested in and include direct links to those postings.
5. **Close the email** — End with a clear call to action and attach your resume.

**End (Reinforcement):** Reiterates that the format is brief, on point, and research-driven. Shares that the job market is competitive and this strategy personally led to multiple interviews.
