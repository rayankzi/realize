## Executive Summary

This reel delivers a high-energy, no-fluff list of strategies for student founders who want to hit the ground running in college. The creator emphasizes treating your startup as the top priority from day one — leveraging campus resources, networking aggressively with alumni, optimizing coursework for minimal distraction, and embedding yourself in the entrepreneurial ecosystem. The caption humorously notes a "99% success rate" with a tongue-in-cheek warning about a "1% chance you get suspended," signaling that these tactics push boundaries but are highly effective.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Declare your priority immediately:** Tell every professor on day one that your startup is your focus — this sets expectations and may earn flexibility.
- **Network relentlessly:** Reach out to at least 10 alumni in your space every single week, and use your .edu email to cold-email potential clients for quick chats.
- **Leverage campus infrastructure:** Apply to every pitch competition, join accelerators or startup programs, and try to become the face of those programs for visibility.
- **Optimize your environment:** Use Rate My Professor to pick low-workload classes, and try to room with someone who is also building something.
- **Be patient with co-founders:** Don't ask someone to co-found with you the first few times you meet — build the relationship first.
- **Build consistency:** Find and stick to a routine that supports both your academics and your startup.
