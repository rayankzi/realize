# 5 Common Interview Questions and How to Answer Them

**Topic/Category:** Career Advice / Interview Prep
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach job seekers how to reframe their answers to five common interview questions — showing what NOT to say and providing polished alternatives.

## Executive Summary

This reel breaks down five of the most frequently asked interview questions and provides a clear "don't say / instead say" framework for each. The core message is that interviews aren't about lying — they're about framing your story in a way that resonates with employers. ## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** When discussing weaknesses, name a real but manageable one and immediately pivot to the steps you're taking to improve — this shows self-awareness and growth mindset.
- **Insight 2:** Never badmouth a previous employer or team when explaining why you left a role. Reframe departures around career alignment and seeking greater responsibility.
- **Insight 3:** For future-oriented questions ("Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"), anchor your answer to the company you're interviewing at — show ambition tied to their growth, not vague aspirations.
- **Insight 4:** Career gaps should be reframed as intentional periods of skill development, freelancing, or personal growth rather than involuntary downtime.
- **Insight 5:** Hobbies and interests are an opportunity to connect personal passions to transferable skills and company culture fit.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

The reel walks through five questions in a consistent format:

1. **"What's your greatest weakness?"** — Avoid giving a non-answer or a disqualifying weakness. Instead, mention a genuine but minor weakness (e.g., getting caught up in details) and show you're actively working on it with concrete tools and strategies.

2. **"Why did you leave your last job?"** — Never cite negativity (bad manager, toxic team). Reframe around seeking better alignment with long-term career goals and wanting more responsibility and challenges.

3. **"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"** — Don't be too vague, but don't over-plan either. Express a desire to grow within the company, take on leadership, and develop skills in a specific area relevant to the role.

4. **"Why is there a career gap on your resume?"** — Don't say you simply couldn't find work. Instead, highlight that you used the time to develop new skills, freelance, and gain insights that make you a stronger candidate.

5. **"What are your hobbies or interests?"** — Don't say you have none or that you only work. Name a real hobby and connect it to relevant skills and company values.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

**Q1: What's your greatest weakness?**

**Q5: What are your hobbies or interests?**
