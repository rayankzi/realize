# How to Get Ahead of 99% of CS Majors

**Topic/Category:** Tech Careers / Computer Science Education
**Primary Goal of the Post:** A 2x Big Tech (FAANG) intern shares a comprehensive playbook of actionable strategies that CS students should adopt early to differentiate themselves from peers and land top internships.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel from creator Ella Gonzales (@itsellagonzales) distills the exact strategies she used to land a Microsoft internship as a sophomore — even though she didn't enter college as a CS major. The post covers nine pillars: building projects early, deprioritizing GPA obsession, applying to exploratory programs, starting interview prep ahead of time, studying smarter, posting on LinkedIn, learning system design, and joining CS communities. The overarching message is that most CS students don't do all of these things, so doing even a handful puts you significantly ahead.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Build Projects Early:** Don't wait for applications to open. A personal portfolio is the ideal first project and can be built in a weekend with AI tools like Claude Code. The "Build Your Own X" GitHub repo is recommended for project ideas.
- **GPA Doesn't Matter (As Much As You Think):** Tech recruiters care far less about GPA than students assume. Never sacrifice interview prep or project work for a test grade — perfect grades with no job is the wrong trade.
- **Do Exploratory Programs & Summits:** These are how you get into the recruiting pipeline early. Companies learn your name, you get their brand on your resume, and you build recruiter relationships before formal recruiting begins.
- **Start Interview Prep Early:** Consistency (20 minutes a day) beats cramming. NeetCode and LeetCode are the two best resources — use both.
- **Study Smarter, Not Harder:** If lectures aren't working, skip them and learn via YouTube. Abdul Bari on YouTube is specifically recommended for data structures.
- **Post on LinkedIn Now:** Don't wait for achievements — share what you're learning, building, and even failing at. Building a brand early leads to recruiter inbound.
- **Learn System Design:** With AI writing most code, system design knowledge is the differentiator. The System Design Primer on GitHub is the top free resource.
- **Join CS Communities:** People are the biggest resource. Product Haven Slack for product-focused roles; cscareers.dev Discord for comprehensive CS career content.
