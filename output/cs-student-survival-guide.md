## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1: Your first year is your biggest window of free time.** Unless you're coming in with a stack of AP credits, the rigorous CS coursework doesn't hit until year two. Use that first-year breathing room strategically — for projects, networking, and skill-building — because the workload only increases.
- **Insight 2: You're not learning to work — you're working to learn.** A CS degree gives you theoretical foundations, but it does NOT prepare you for actual software engineering, data engineering, or industry work. You have to actively learn modern frameworks and technologies on your own time.
- **Insight 3: Trade some GPA points for hands-on experience.** If it means studying one hour less to do one hour more of LeetCode or project work, do it. You're not LeBron (the kid with a 4.0 and a FAANG internship as a freshman) — you're more like Michael Beasley, and that's fine. "Reach for the stars, land on the moon" is enough to get an internship.
- **Insight 4: Stop hermiting — go hang out with your friends.** Higher dopamine and lower cortisol from socializing actually makes you lock in harder when you need to. Saying yes to Saturday night hangouts makes you more efficient overall, not less. There are times to grind, but it shouldn't be every single weekend.
