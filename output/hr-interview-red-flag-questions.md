# 7 Questions HR Hopes You Never Ask (But You Definitely Should)

**Topic/Category:** Career / Job Interviews
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To arm job candidates with seven powerful interview questions that expose toxic cultures, fake growth roles, and high-turnover companies — so they can make informed career decisions before accepting an offer.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel by Shulin Lee (@theshulinlee) presents seven strategic questions candidates should ask during job interviews to uncover red flags that HR and hiring managers typically hope go unasked. Each question targets a specific aspect of the role or company — from why the position is open, to how decisions are made, to what happened to the previous person in the role. The caption reinforces the urgency: most candidates accept blindly and regret loudly. The post encourages viewers to ask better questions to protect their careers.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Most candidates are too passive in interviews.** They don't ask probing questions and end up accepting roles they later regret.
- **Specific questions can reveal hidden dysfunction.** Each of the seven questions is designed to surface a particular type of red flag — revolving-door turnover, lack of ownership, unrealistic expectations, or a toxic culture.
- **How a company responds is as important as what they say.** The post emphasizes watching for dodging, hesitation, vagueness, and deflection as warning signs.
- **Preparation is protection.** Asking these questions before accepting an offer can save you from hidden toxic cultures, dead-end roles, and companies that churn through people.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

- **Slide 1 — Cover:** "7 Questions HR Hopes You Never Ask (but you definitely should)" — sets the hook with a bold, curiosity-driven title. Features Shulin Lee.

- **Slide 2 — Question 1: "Why is this role open?"**
  - Red flags: They dodge the question; "The last person wasn't a good fit" (3rd time in 2 years); They can't give a straight answer.
  - This uncovers: Is this a revolving door?

- **Slide 3 — Question 2: "Can I speak to someone who recently joined?"**
  - Watch out for: "That's not really possible"; They hesitate; They offer only senior leadership.
  - This uncovers: What are they hiding?

- **Slide 4 — Question 3: "What does success look like in 6 months?"**
  - Watch out for: They can't define it; Vague answers; Expectations sound impossible.
  - This reveals: Will I be set up to win or fail?

- **Slide 5 — Question 4: "How are decisions made here?"**
  - Warning signs: "It depends"; Everything needs 10 approvals; One person controls everything.
  - This helps you understand: Will I have any real ownership?

- **Slide 6 — Question 5: "What happened to the last person in this role?"**
  - Red flags: Awkward silence; "They moved on" with zero context; They've had 3 people in 2 years.
  - This helps you see: Am I walking into a mess?

- **Slide 7 — Summary:** Take the time to prepare. The right questions protect you from hidden toxic cultures, roles with no real path forward, and companies that churn through people. Ask these BEFORE you accept.
