# The Internship Resume Template That Actually Works

**Topic/Category:** Career / Resume Building
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To share a proven resume template structure that has helped the creator land 10+ internships, offering students a reusable framework for writing effective resumes.

---

## Executive Summary

This two-slide Instagram carousel presents a resume template from a Cornell Law student and Forbes 30 Under 30 early career educator who has completed 10 internships. The post promises a practical, fill-in-the-blank resume format designed specifically for college students seeking internships. The template emphasizes structured bullet points with measurable outcomes, a clean single-page layout, and a results-oriented writing formula.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Results-oriented bullet formula:** Every bullet point follows a consistent structure: action verb + specific task/scope + measurable outcome + timeframe + method. This forces the writer to quantify their impact rather than just list duties.
- **Three bullets per role:** The template uses exactly three bullet points per experience entry, each with a distinct purpose: (1) leading a task to a measurable result, (2) collaboration and ownership, (3) identifying a problem and taking initiative.
- **Education section comes first:** For students, education is placed at the top, including GPA, relevant coursework, student organizations, and honors/awards — maximizing the most relevant credentials.
- **Clean, single-page format:** The template is minimal and ATS-friendly with clear section headers (Education, Experience, Skills) and no graphics or columns that could confuse applicant tracking systems.
- **Four experience entries:** The template accommodates four separate roles, suggesting students should aim to have multiple experiences (internships, campus involvement, projects) even early in their careers.

**Slide 2: The Resume Template**
A full-page resume template is shown with the following structure:

1. **Header:** Firstname Lastname, phone number, city/state, school email, LinkedIn (hyperlinked)
2. **Education:** College name, major, minor, GPA, anticipated grad date, student org titles, relevant coursework, honors & awards
3. **Experience (x4 entries):** Each entry includes:
   - Title + Place of Work/Involvement + City, State + Date Range
   - Bullet 1: "I [verb] [specific task + scope], leading to [measurable outcome + number] within [timeframe], through [method or approach]."
   - Bullet 2: "Worked alongside [who] to [what you did together], taking ownership of [your specific piece] which [resulted in what]."
   - Bullet 3: "Identified [specific problem or gap], took initiative to [what you did about it], which led to [measurable outcome] within [timeframe]."
4. **Skills:** Listed horizontally, separated by pipes (XYZ | XYZ | XYZ...)
