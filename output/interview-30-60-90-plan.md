# The 30/60/90 Day Plan That Gets You Hired

**Topic/Category:** Career Strategy / Interview Prep
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach job seekers a proactive post-interview strategy — creating a 30/60/90 day plan presentation — to stand out from other candidates and demonstrate initiative.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel shares a high-impact interview hack: after each round of interviews, put together a short 3–4 slide presentation outlining a 30/60/90 day plan for the role. The plan should detail what you'd do in your first 30 days, what you'd tackle by 60 days, and where you'd aim to be at 90 days — including specific projects you believe the team could take on. The creator emphasizes that even if your proposals are off-base, the act of going above and beyond signals proactivity and initiative far more effectively than simply claiming you're a hard worker.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Show, don't tell:** Instead of saying you make great presentations or are proactive, actually demonstrate it by delivering a plan unprompted.
- **The 30/60/90 framework:** Structure your post-interview deliverable around three milestones — 30 days (onboarding/learning), 60 days (contributing/fixing), and 90 days (shipping/leading).
- **Accuracy matters less than effort:** You could be completely wrong about the team's priorities, and that's okay. What hiring managers are really evaluating is whether you're the kind of person who goes above and beyond.
- **Proactivity is the differentiator:** The biggest signal employers look for is initiative — and this tactic proves it in a way that words alone cannot.

## Narrative & Step-by-Step Breakdown

**Beginning (Hook):** The reel opens with a question — "After each of these interviews, is there something that we should be doing?" — immediately framing the advice as an actionable, often-overlooked step in the interview process.

**Middle (Core Strategy):**
1. After each interview round, create a concise 3–4 slide presentation.
2. Frame it as "Here's exactly what I would do if I were to join this company."
3. Use the 30/60/90 structure:
   - **First 30 days:** What you'd learn, observe, and begin working on.
   - **60 days:** What you'd fix, improve, or contribute to.
   - **90 days:** What you'd ship or lead.
4. Include specific project ideas you believe would be impactful for the team.

**End (Key Takeaway):** The creator acknowledges you might be wrong about the specifics, but reinforces that the exercise itself demonstrates willingness to go above and beyond — which is the real thing interviewers are looking for. The closing line drives it home: "We'll actually show them."
