# 5 Things You Should Technically "Lie" About in Job Interviews

**Topic/Category:** Career / Interview Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Teach job seekers how to strategically reframe sensitive interview topics — like reasons for leaving, future plans, and hobbies — so they sound professional, confident, and hireable without being dishonest.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel from @resumeguru.in tackles one of the most uncomfortable realities of job interviews: you can't always say exactly what you're thinking. Rather than advocating for dishonesty, the post teaches candidates how to reframe five common interview topics — your 5-year plan, reason for leaving, opinions on past bosses/coworkers, hobbies, and job title — in ways that project maturity, stability, and professionalism. The core message is that interviews reward strategic communication, not raw honesty, and the way you frame your answers determines how hireable you appear.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Framing matters more than raw honesty:** Interviews are about presenting the right version of the truth — staying honest while positioning yourself as confident, professional, and aligned with the company's needs.
- **Never badmouth past employers or colleagues:** Even if the environment was toxic, complaining signals negativity. Redirect toward enthusiasm for new opportunities.
- **Future plans should signal loyalty and growth:** Personal milestones (marriage, school) are valid life goals but don't belong in an interview answer about your 5-year plan. Keep it role-focused.
- **Your job title is less important than your actual contributions:** If your title doesn't match your real responsibilities, lead with the work you did, not what it was called.
- **Hobbies are a character signal:** Generic answers like "watching Netflix" communicate nothing. Choose hobbies that reflect discipline, curiosity, or personality — or pivot the conversation.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

- **Slide 1 — Cover/Hook:** A photo of the creator with bold text: "5 things you should technically 'lie' about in job interview!" — designed to stop the scroll with a provocative but relatable hook.
- **Slide 2 — Your 5-Year Plan:** Don't mention personal goals like getting married, starting a family, or going back to school. Instead say: *"I see myself growing with this organization and taking on bigger responsibilities."*
- **Slide 3 — Why You're Leaving Your Job:** Never say you hate the culture or your manager. Instead say: *"I've outgrown my current role and I'm looking for a new challenge."*
- **Slide 4 — Your Boss or Coworkers:** Even if they were toxic, don't complain. Instead say: *"I'm excited to work with a new team and learn in a different environment."*
- **Slide 5 — Your Hobbies:** "Watching Netflix" isn't doing you any favors. Mention something more engaging or just pivot the conversation.
- **Slide 6 — Your Job Title:** If your title doesn't reflect what you actually do, focus on the work and responsibilities, not the outdated title.
