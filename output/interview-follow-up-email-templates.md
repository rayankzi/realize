# The Follow-Up Email That Got Me the Job

**Topic/Category:** Career / Interview Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Provide a strategic 3-step follow-up email timeline with exact templates to send after a job interview, helping candidates stay top of mind without being pushy.

---

## Executive Summary

Most candidates fall into one of two traps after an interview: they either never follow up (fearing they'll seem desperate) or they follow up excessively (actually being pushy). This carousel from @sixfigureea lays out a precise 3-step follow-up framework with copy-paste email templates timed at 24 hours, one week, and two weeks post-interview. The core message is that strategic follow-up demonstrates professionalism, genuine interest, and initiative -- the exact traits hiring managers look for.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Following up isn't desperate -- silence is.** A well-timed follow-up signals continued interest, proactiveness, and professionalism.
- **There's a strategic middle ground** between never following up and overdoing it. The framework provides exactly three touchpoints with decreasing urgency.
- **Each email serves a different purpose:** the first reinforces fit, the second checks in politely, and the third gracefully acknowledges timing while leaving the door open.
- **Following up keeps you top of mind** -- interviewers meet 10+ candidates, and a follow-up helps you stand out while demonstrating initiative.
- **You gain clarity** instead of waiting in limbo, regardless of the outcome.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

### Step 1 -- The Immediate Thank You (Within 24 Hours)

> **Subject:** Thank you -- [Position] Interview
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the [Position] role. I really enjoyed learning about [specific detail from conversation] and how the team approaches [relevant topic].
>
> Our conversation really strengthened my excitement to bring my [relevant skill] to the team.
>
> Please let me know if you need anything else from me in the meantime.
>
> Looking forward to hearing from you.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Why it works: Timely, specific, reinforces fit.*

### Step 2 -- The One-Week Check-In (If You Haven't Heard Back)

> **Subject:** Following up -- [Position] Interview
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I wanted to follow up on my interview for the [Position] role. I remain very interested in the opportunity and would love to know if there are any updates on next steps.
>
> If there's anything else you need from me, references, work samples, or additional information, I'm happy to provide it.
>
> Thank you again for your time and consideration.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Why it works: Polite, brief, offers to help move things forward.*

### Step 3 -- The Two-Week Nudge (If Still Silence)

> **Subject:** Checking in -- [Position] Opportunity
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I wanted to check in one more time regarding the [Position] role. I understand hiring timelines can shift, and I remain very interested if the opportunity is still open.
>
> If you've moved forward with other candidates, I completely understand and would appreciate knowing so I can plan accordingly.
>
> Either way, thank you for considering me.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Why it works: Acknowledges their timeline, gives them an easy out, maintains professionalism.*
