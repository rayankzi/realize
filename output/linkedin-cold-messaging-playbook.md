# How to Cold Message Recruiters on LinkedIn (And Actually Get Replies)

**Topic/Category:** Career / Job Search / LinkedIn Networking
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To teach students and job seekers the exact playbook for writing LinkedIn cold messages that actually get read and replied to — covering structure, targeting, timing, and common mistakes to avoid.

---

## Executive Summary

Most students blast 50+ generic LinkedIn messages and hear nothing back — not because recruiters are rude, but because the messages lack context, personalization, and a clear ask. This 13-slide carousel by @skb (Sai Kumar Bysani / datawithsai) lays out a complete cold messaging framework: how to craft a strong first line, structure your message in four parts (Context → Relevance → Reason → Ask), choose the right people to message, time your follow-ups, and optimize your profile so it backs up your outreach. It also includes ready-to-use copy-paste templates for different recipient types.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Recruiters receive 100+ messages per week** — they scan in seconds and only reply when messages show clarity, relevance, and effort. Professional beats desperate every time.
- **Emotional/begging messages are the #1 mistake.** Lines like "Hi sir please help me get a job" or "Can you give me referral" get ignored. Thoughtful, research-backed messages get replies.
- **Your first line is the most important part.** Use the formula: Context + Personalization + Reason for reaching out.
- **The perfect message has 4 parts:** (1) Context, (2) Relevance, (3) Reason for message, (4) Clear ask.
- **Don't only message HR.** Smart job seekers also reach out to recruiters, team managers, senior analysts, alumni from target companies, and hiring managers.
- **Strong asks are specific, not vague.** "What skills should freshers focus on for your team?" beats "Please help me get job."
- **Timing matters.** Send on weekdays, follow up after 5–7 days, keep follow-ups short. Never spam daily or sound impatient.
- **Your profile must match your message.** Recruiters always check your profile before replying — ensure a clear headline, listed projects, visible skills, and a professional photo.
- **A 5-point checklist before sending** puts you ahead of 90% of job seekers.

## Verbatim Templates & Scripts

**Template 1 — For Recruiters:**

> "Hi [Name], I noticed you hire for [Role] at [Company]. I'm a [background] with hands-on experience in [skills]. Would love to know what skills you prioritize in freshers. Happy to share my portfolio too."

**Template 2 — For Hiring Managers:**

> "Hi [Name], your recent post on [topic] was really insightful. I'm a [background] exploring [role] opportunities and your team's work stood out. Could I share my portfolio for a quick feedback?"

**Strong First Line Examples:**

> "I saw your post about hiring analysts at Infosys..."
>
> "Noticed you lead campus hiring at Deloitte..."
>
> "Your recent article on data careers was helpful..."
>
> "I'm a final-year student exploring analyst roles..."

**Strong Ask Examples:**

> "What skills should freshers focus on for your team?"
>
> "Can I share my portfolio for quick feedback?"
>
> "Would you recommend applying through referrals?"
>
> "Is your team currently hiring entry-level analysts?"

**Thoughtful Message Examples:**

> "I noticed you hire for Data Analyst roles at TCS"
>
> "I've worked on SQL & Power BI projects relevant to your team"
>
> "Would love to know what skills you prioritize in freshers"
>
> "Can I share my portfolio for your feedback?"
