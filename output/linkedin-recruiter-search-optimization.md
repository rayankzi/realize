# How to Optimize LinkedIn to Get 60+ Recruiter DMs as a PM

**Topic/Category:** Career / LinkedIn Optimization
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach viewers how to strategically optimize their LinkedIn profile so recruiters find and message them organically, rather than relying on outbound job applications.

## Executive Summary

This reel shares a practical, keyword-driven approach to LinkedIn optimization from a Product Manager working in New York City. The creator flips the traditional job search paradigm — instead of chasing companies, they engineered their profile to attract over 60 recruiter DMs since August. The strategy revolves around reverse-engineering recruiter search behavior, extending job titles with high-value keywords, and saturating the profile with adjacent terms across multiple sections.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Recruiters search LinkedIn using specific keywords (e.g., "API experience") — your profile needs to contain those exact terms to appear in their search results.
- **Insight 2:** Extending your job title beyond the generic (e.g., "Product Manager → Product Manager API Experience") dramatically increases search visibility for niche roles.
- **Insight 3:** Adding adjacent/related keywords (e.g., RESTful API, webhooks, Postman alongside "API experience") broadens the net of recruiter searches you'll appear in.
- **Insight 4:** Keywords should be distributed across multiple profile sections — headline, about, skills, projects, and experience — not just one place.
- **Insight 5:** Linking LinkedIn skills directly to specific experience entries further boosts relevance in recruiter searches.
