# Stop Rambling on Networking Calls — The 80/20 Rule for Better Conversations

**Topic/Category:** Career Networking / Internship & Job Search Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach students and job seekers how to rebalance their networking calls so the other person does most of the talking, leading to stronger impressions and better outcomes.

---

## Executive Summary

Most people approach networking calls wrong — they talk too much. This reel (Day 19 of a 300-day series on landing internships and full-time jobs) argues that the best networking conversations follow a 20-25% you / 75-80% them split. The creator, who has helped hundreds of students get offers and grown a 20,000+ follower LinkedIn audience through networking, explains that human nature favors people who make conversations feel easy by letting others talk about themselves.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Talk less, listen more:** The ideal networking call ratio is 20-25% you talking, 75-80% them talking. Most people do the opposite.
- **People like talking about themselves:** This is the fundamental psychology behind why letting them lead the conversation creates a better impression.
- **Preparation beats improvisation:** Create a targeted list of questions before every call so you can steer the conversation toward their experience without scrambling.
- **Avoid unnecessary elaboration:** Answer their questions directly and concisely — don't ramble or over-explain.
- **The best impression is effortlessness:** You "win" a networking call when the other person walks away feeling like the conversation was easy and enjoyable.
