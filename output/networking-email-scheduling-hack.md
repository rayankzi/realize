# Stop Asking "What Works For You?" in Networking Emails

**Topic/Category:** Career Networking / Job Search Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** Teach viewers a specific scheduling tactic that increases response rates when sending networking or coffee chat emails.

---

## Executive Summary

This reel, part of a 300-day series on landing internships and full-time jobs, explains why asking "let me know when works for you" in networking emails actually hurts your response rate. The creator — who has helped hundreds of students get offers and built a 20,000+ follower LinkedIn network — argues that open-ended availability questions create friction. Instead, you should provide three specific time ranges and send the calendar invite yourself, making it as easy as possible for the other person to say yes.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Never ask for open-ended availability.** Saying "let me know what works for you" forces the recipient to do extra work, which lowers your response rate — not because they don't want to help, but because it adds friction.
- **Offer three structured time ranges.** Give concrete options (e.g., Tuesday 10–12, Wednesday 1–3, Thursday at 11) so the recipient can simply pick one.
- **Send the calendar invite yourself.** Don't make the other person handle logistics. Taking ownership of the invite removes another barrier to getting a "yes."
