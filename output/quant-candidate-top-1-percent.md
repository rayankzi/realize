## Executive Summary

This reel breaks down five critical differentiators that top quant hedge fund candidates share — none of which are about having a perfect GPA or the "right" degree. ## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1: Original work trumps credentials.** Elite funds auto-reject candidates whose GitHub profiles consist of forks and tutorials. They want proof of building from scratch before anyone told you to — a signal of genuine obsession, not compliance.
- **Insight 2: Competition medals are proxy signals for on-the-job performance.** Over 45% of hires at elite funds had Math Olympiad or equivalent medals. These prove the ability to think under pressure, alone, on novel problems — which is literally the daily job description.
- **Insight 3: Resilience after failure is a hiring signal.** The top 1% had already failed publicly (bad models, bombed competitions) and came back. Firms hire people who won't freeze when markets punish certainty.
- **Insight 4: Self-directed obsession is non-negotiable.** The best candidates weren't following syllabi — they were building, contributing to open source, and researching at 2am out of genuine interest. No one hands you a signal in quant; you find it yourself.
- **Insight 5: Being memorable matters more than being safe.** Mental math and brainteasers are elimination filters. What earns the offer is being genuinely interesting to talk to — not louder or funnier, but authentically engaged and curious.
