# Everything You Need to Know for Quant Finance

**Topic/Category:** Quant Finance / Career Roadmap
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To provide a comprehensive overview of the five core knowledge pillars required to break into and succeed in quantitative finance.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel breaks down the essential skill areas needed for a career in quantitative finance into five clear categories: Math, Statistics & Modeling, Coding, AI/ML, and Finance Knowledge. The post serves as a personal roadmap the creator is following, offering aspiring quants a structured checklist of topics to study across each domain. It emphasizes that quant finance sits at the intersection of deep mathematical rigor, programming proficiency, machine learning expertise, and practical financial market understanding.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Insight 1:** Quant finance requires a strong mathematical foundation spanning calculus, linear algebra, differential equations (ODE, PDE, SDE), probability, stochastic processes, optimization, and numerical methods.
- **Insight 2:** Statistical modeling skills — including regression, inference, time-series analysis, regularization, dimensionality reduction, and statistical reasoning — are essential for building and validating quantitative models.
- **Insight 3:** Programming proficiency should cover both scripting languages (Python, R, MATLAB) and production-oriented languages (C/C++), with Julia recommended as a versatile newer option.
- **Insight 4:** AI/ML knowledge focuses on neural networks, tree-based models, and gradient boosting models, combined with a solid understanding of linear algebra to support these techniques.
- **Insight 5:** Domain-specific finance knowledge — including financial markets, asset classes, derivatives, risk, portfolio concepts, and practical trading — rounds out the quant skill set.
