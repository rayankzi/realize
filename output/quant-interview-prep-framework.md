## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Interview question practice is the single highest-leverage activity. Quant interviews test structured reasoning under pressure — not formula recall — so drilling probability puzzles, combinatorics, and brainteasers builds pattern recognition faster than passive study.
- **Insight 2:** Probability fundamentals (conditional reasoning, expectation, independence, order-based thinking) matter far more than jumping into advanced calculus. These concepts appear constantly in both interviews and trading logic.
- **Insight 3:** Python is the experimentation layer. Simulating coin flips, dice, Poisson arrivals, and Monte Carlo price paths turns abstract intuition into concrete understanding of distributions.
- **Insight 4:** Understanding market microstructure (liquidity, spreads, execution costs, risk limits) must come before attempting to build trading strategies — strategies are meaningless outside real constraints.
- **Insight 5:** Speed and communication are trainable skills. Mental math, estimation, and explaining reasoning clearly under time pressure all improve with deliberate repetition.
