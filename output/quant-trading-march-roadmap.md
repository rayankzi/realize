## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Insight 1:** Interview prep is the highest-ROI activity — focus on timed problem-solving in probability, combinatorics, conditional reasoning, and expected value rather than passive reading. Target 30–45 minutes daily minimum.
- **Insight 2:** Study real economic events (CPI, FOMC, payroll, earnings) not to predict outcomes, but to model implied distributions of outcomes, volatility shifts, and conditional probabilities. Think in scenarios and regimes, not opinions.
- **Insight 3:** Build Python projects grounded in randomness — start with coin flips and dice simulations, then progress to Monte Carlo models using geometric Brownian motion. Focus on understanding volatility, drift, and randomness.
- **Insight 4:** Develop intuition for linear algebra concepts like covariance matrices, correlation structure in portfolio risk, and eigenvalues in PCA — you need practical intuition about factor exposure, not formal proofs.
- **Insight 5:** Train mental math, log comparisons, and exponent approximations for speed. Quant interviews are timed and markets are fast — precision under constraint must be practiced deliberately.
