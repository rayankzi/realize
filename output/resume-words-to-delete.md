# Words On Your Resume Getting You Rejected: What to Delete and What to Use Instead

**Topic/Category:** Career / Resume Tips
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To help job seekers identify and replace weak, vague language on their resumes with strong, results-oriented action verbs that demonstrate impact and ownership.

---

## Executive Summary

Most resumes are filled with generic, passive phrases like "responsible for" and "team player" that sound professional but communicate nothing meaningful to hiring managers. This carousel from @SixFigureEA breaks down exactly which words to delete from your resume and provides specific, powerful replacements that emphasize action, ownership, and measurable results. The core message: HR doesn't reward effort — they reward impact.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Vague language hurts your chances:** Words that sound professional but lack specificity (e.g., "responsible for," "hardworking," "assisted") make your resume forgettable and can actively lead to rejection.
- **HR rewards impact, not effort:** Hiring managers are looking for evidence of results and ownership, not descriptions of duties or self-assessed character traits.
- **Action verbs signal ownership:** Swapping passive phrases for strong verbs like "Led," "Executed," "Spearheaded," and "Delivered" immediately changes how your experience is perceived.
- **Numbers turn duties into value:** Adding quantifiable results (e.g., "increasing retention by 25%") transforms a generic job description into a compelling achievement.
- **Your resume should sound valuable, not busy:** The goal is to communicate the value you delivered, not just the volume of tasks you performed.
