# Never Say This When Giving Your Availability

**Topic/Category:** Career / Internship & Job Search Strategy
**Primary Goal of the Reel:** To warn job seekers and students against offering overly wide availability windows when scheduling coffee chats and interviews, because it signals you have nothing going on.

---

## Executive Summary

When scheduling interviews or coffee chats, offering a massive availability window (e.g., "Monday to Friday, 9 to 5") is a red flag to recruiters and professionals. It signals that you have nothing else going on in your life—no projects, clubs, or commitments. Instead, you should anchor your schedule around real obligations (classes, meals, activities) and offer specific, narrower time slots that make you appear busy and in-demand. This is part of a 300-day series on why candidates fail to land internships and full-time roles.

## Core Insights & Takeaways

- **Wide availability windows kill your impression:** Saying "I'm free Monday through Thursday" or "available 9–5 Monday to Friday" makes the recipient assume you sit on the couch all day.
- **Busy people attract opportunities:** Professionals want to network with and hire people who are actively engaged in projects, clubs, sports, and other activities.
- **Anchor your schedule around real commitments:** Even if your calendar is light, structure your availability around classes, meals, or other obligations to appear organized and in-demand.
- **This applies even more to interviews than coffee chats:** While it matters for networking, the stakes are higher when scheduling formal interviews where first impressions count.
