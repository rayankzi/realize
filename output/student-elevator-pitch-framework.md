# The 3-Line Student Elevator Pitch Framework

**Topic/Category:** Career Development / Networking
**Primary Goal of the Post:** Teach students a simple 3-line pitch formula that positions them as action-oriented and memorable when networking — even without prior experience.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel from @internshipgirl breaks down a concise 3-line elevator pitch formula designed specifically for students who feel they have "nothing to offer." Rather than relying on generic introductions like "I'm a [major] student at [school]," the framework reframes the pitch around action, purpose, and a specific ask. The creator — a 10x intern, Cornell Law student, and Forbes 30u30 early career educator — presents each line with a "skip this / say this instead" format, showing how small language shifts can dramatically change how a student is perceived at networking events and in professional settings.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Lead with action, not identity:** Instead of stating your school and major, open with your name, what you study, and what you're actively building, researching, or leading. This shifts perception from passive learner to young leader.
- **Replace "passion" with a real origin story:** Rather than saying "I've always been passionate about XYZ," share a specific moment or observation that made you committed to your field. This makes you memorable and gives you a genuine point of view.
- **Define your edge and close with a direct ask:** Instead of vaguely saying "I'm a hard worker," name a specific skill that sets you apart and directly ask about opportunities. Ending with "No tasks are too big or too small. I just want to learn" signals humility and willingness.
- **You don't need experience to pitch yourself:** If you have no formal experience, lean into what excites you in your coursework — specific topics from your classes count as legitimate interests.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

**Slide 1 (Cover):** Title card — "How to pitch yourself as a student (when you feel like you have nothing to offer)" — with the creator's credentials: 10x intern, Cornell Law student, Forbes 30u30 early career educator.

**Slide 2 — LINE 1: An opening that leads with action.**
- Skip: "I'm a [major] student at [school]."
- Say instead: "I'm [Name]. I study [X] and I'm currently [building / researching / leading] [specific thing]."
- If no experience, focus on specific topics in your major classes that interest you most.
- *Effect: Passive learner → young leader with ideas and momentum.*

**Slide 3 — LINE 2: Your deeper "why?"**
- Skip: "I've always been passionate about XYZ." (Overused.)
- Say instead: "I care about [X] because I've seen what happens when no one does [real, specific reason]."
- You only need one origin moment that made you committed — not a lineup of internships.
- *Effect: One of many networkers → someone with a memorable point of view.*

**Slide 4 — LINE 3: Define your edge + make the ask.**
- Skip: "I'm a hard worker."
- Say instead: "Through my experiences, I've become the person who [specific skill that sets you apart in a team]. I'd love to bring that to your work at [XYZ]. Are there opportunities for a student like me to join your team? No tasks are too big or too small. I just want to learn."
- *Effect: Average student asking to shadow → someone who knows their value and has something real to give.*
