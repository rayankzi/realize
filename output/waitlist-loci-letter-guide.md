# How to Write the Perfect Letter of Continued Interest (LOCI)

**Topic/Category:** College Admissions / Education
**Primary Goal of the Post:** To provide a step-by-step framework for writing a Letter of Continued Interest that can help waitlisted students get accepted, using the creator's successful Duke University experience as proof.

---

## Executive Summary

This carousel breaks down the exact paragraph-by-paragraph structure of a Letter of Continued Interest (LOCI) — the letter waitlisted college applicants send to admissions committees to reaffirm their desire to attend. The creator shares the framework that helped her move from Duke University's waitlist to an acceptance, walking through four key paragraphs covering gratitude, expanded "Why School" reasoning, extracurricular updates, and a strong closing expression of enthusiasm.

## Key Insights & Main Ideas

- **Insight 1:** A LOCI should open by thanking the admissions committee, setting a respectful and appreciative tone before diving into substance.
- **Insight 2:** The most critical element is expanding on your "Why This School" essay with new, specific reasons — such as reaching out to professors or visiting campus — that demonstrate genuine continued interest.
- **Insight 3:** Including 1-2 extracurricular updates with new achievements or milestones shows growth and momentum since the original application.
- **Insight 4:** The letter should close concisely by mentioning recent achievements, expressing genuine excitement about attending, and signing off professionally.

## Detailed Breakdown / Step-by-Step

**Slide 1: Introduction & Hook**
The creator introduces the topic — "How to write the perfect Letter of Continued Interest" — and establishes credibility by sharing that her letter got her from the waitlist to accepted at Duke University.

**Slide 2: Paragraphs 1 & 2**
- Thank the admissions committee for their consideration.
- Expand/elaborate on your "Why [School]" with a more specific reason. The creator's example: she talked about reaching out to and getting a lesson with a Duke cello professor during the spring of her senior year.

**Slide 3: Paragraph 3**
- Elaborate on 1-2 different extracurricular activities, preferably with new achievements or updates since you submitted your original application.

**Slide 4: Paragraph 4**
- Briefly mention recent achievements.
- Express your excitement to attend the school if accepted.
- Sign off professionally.
